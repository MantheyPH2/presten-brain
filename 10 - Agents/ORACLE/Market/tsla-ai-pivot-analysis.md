# Tesla's AI Pivot -- Is TSLA an AI Stock Now?

> Dan Ives of Wedbush declared Tesla is "more an AI company than a car company." TSLA just guided $25B in capex. ORACLE breaks down what this means, where the money is going, and whether the market should re-rate TSLA as an AI stock.

---

## The Dan Ives Thesis

Dan Ives is one of the most influential Tesla analysts on Wall Street (Wedbush Securities). His statement -- "Tesla is now more an AI company than a car company" -- is not a casual remark. It is a deliberate narrative reframing that has real implications for how TSLA should be valued.

### What He's Actually Saying
1. Tesla's future value accrues primarily from AI-driven businesses (FSD, Robotaxi, Optimus) not from selling cars
2. The $25B capex is an AI infrastructure investment, comparable to what hyperscalers spend
3. The correct valuation framework for TSLA is tech/AI multiples, not auto P/E
4. The EPS beat ($0.41 vs $0.35) and margin expansion (21.7% vs 17.7%) prove the car business is funding the AI pivot without diluting shareholders

### Why It Matters
If the market accepts the Ives reframing:
- **Auto P/E:** TSLA at ~95x forward earnings (current) looks absurd for a car company
- **AI/Tech P/E:** TSLA at ~95x forward earnings looks normal-to-cheap compared to NVDA (~55x), or companies with optionality on massive TAMs

The question is whether the market SHOULD accept it. That depends on where $25B is actually going.

---

## $25B CapEx -- Where Is the Money Going?

Tesla guided $25B in 2026 capex, up from ~$20B prior guidance and ~$11B actual in 2025. This is a 127% increase YoY. Here is the likely allocation based on management commentary, analyst estimates, and ORACLE's analysis:

### Estimated CapEx Breakdown

| Category | Estimated Allocation | Purpose |
|----------|---------------------|---------|
| **AI Compute / Dojo** | $6-8B | Training compute for FSD, Optimus, and AI models. Custom Dojo supercomputer scaling. NVIDIA GPU purchases for training clusters. |
| **Megafactory Expansion** | $5-7B | New Megapack manufacturing facilities. Energy storage was 8.8 GWh vs 12.45 GWh expected -- the miss suggests supply constraints that capex is meant to fix. |
| **Robotaxi / Cybercab Infrastructure** | $4-6B | Manufacturing lines for Cybercab. Mapping/fleet infrastructure. Charging network for autonomous fleet. |
| **Automotive Plant Upgrades** | $3-4B | Next-gen vehicle platform. Model refresh tooling. Cybertruck ramp. |
| **Optimus Robot** | $2-3B | Humanoid robot R&D and pilot manufacturing lines. Target: 1M units by 2027. |
| **Other (FSD, software, misc)** | $1-2B | FSD compute in vehicles, over-the-air infrastructure, general corporate. |

### The Key Insight
$12-17B of the $25B (roughly 50-70%) is going to AI-adjacent businesses: AI compute, Robotaxi, and Optimus. Only $3-4B is going to traditional automotive. **By capex allocation, Dan Ives is correct -- Tesla is spending like an AI company, not a car company.**

### The Risk
Spending like an AI company does not make you an AI company. The capex only creates value if the products work:
- **Dojo:** Has underdelivered relative to Musk's claims. Still unproven at scale vs. NVIDIA GPUs.
- **Robotaxi:** No revenue yet. Regulatory approval uncertain. Timeline has slipped repeatedly.
- **Optimus:** Pre-commercial. 1M units by 2027 is a Musk timeline, which historically means 2029-2030.

| 2019 | "1M robotaxis on the road by 2020" | Did not happen |
| 2020 | "FSD will be feature-complete this year" | Still incomplete in 2026 |

### Honest Assessment
Musk's Robotaxi timelines have been wrong by 2-4 years, every time. The mid-2026 target is the current official guidance. Based on the historical pattern, a realistic launch for meaningful Robotaxi revenue is **2027-2028.**

However, there is an important nuance: FSD v13 is materially better than previous versions. Autonomous capabilities are genuinely improving. The question is not whether Tesla CAN build a robotaxi -- it is whether regulatory approval and consumer trust can scale to produce meaningful revenue by 2026-2027.

### Revenue Potential (If It Works)
- Robotaxi TAM: $5-10 trillion globally (ride-hailing + logistics + delivery)
- Tesla's addressable share: 10-20% in optimistic scenarios
- Revenue potential: $500B-$2T annually at maturity
- Timeline to meaningful revenue: 2028-2030 in the bull case

The optionality is real. The timeline is not.

## Is TSLA an AI Stock Now? Valuation Framework

### Current Valuation
- Market cap: ~$1.30T
- Trailing EPS (annualized from $0.41 Q1): ~$1.64
- Trailing P/E: ~793x
- Forward P/E (consensus ~$2.00): ~650x
- Forward P/E (if AI-driven margins sustain ~$2.50): ~520x

### Comparison: AI Company Valuations

| Company | Market Cap | 2026E P/E | AI Revenue (est.) | AI CapEx (2026) |
|---------|-----------|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| NVDA | ~$5.2T | ~55x | ~$130B | ~$10-12B |
| MSFT | ~$3.5T | ~35x | ~$50-60B (Azure AI) | ~$55-60B |
| AMZN | ~$2.2T | ~40x | ~$40-50B (AWS AI) | ~$75-80B |
| GOOGL | ~$2.3T | ~25x | ~$40-50B (Cloud AI, ads) | ~$60-65B |
| META | ~$1.6T | ~25x | ~$60B (Advantage+) | ~$40-45B |
| **TSLA** | **~$1.3T** | **~650x** | **~$0 (pre-revenue AI)** | **$25B** |

### The Brutal Truth
Every company in the AI peer group has TENS OF BILLIONS in actual AI revenue. Tesla has approximately ZERO in pure AI revenue. FSD subscriptions contribute, but they are a rounding error relative to the automotive business.

Tesla's AI valuation is based entirely on future optionality:
- Robotaxi: $0 revenue today, $500B+ TAM if it works
- Optimus: $0 revenue today, potentially larger than the car business if it works
- FSD licensing: $0 revenue from licensing today, potentially massive if other automakers adopt

### The Right Framework
TSLA should not be valued as a pure AI company (it has no AI revenue) or a pure auto company (its spending and strategy are AI-focused). The correct framework is:

**TSLA = Auto Business (valued at auto multiples) + AI Optionality (valued as call options on Robotaxi, Optimus, FSD licensing)**

- **Auto business:** ~$100B revenue, ~20% margins, ~$20B operating income. At 15x (generous auto multiple) = $300B
- **AI optionality:** The remaining ~$1T of market cap is the option premium the market pays for Robotaxi + Optimus + FSD licensing
- **Is $1T of option premium justified?** Only if you believe the probability of Robotaxi success x its potential value exceeds $1T in risk-adjusted present value. At a 30% probability of Robotaxi generating $100B+ annual revenue by 2030 with a 20x multiple, the option value is roughly $600B. Add Optimus optionality at a 15% probability and the total approaches $1T.

**Bottom line:** The current $1.3T valuation REQUIRES a combined 25-35% probability that Robotaxi AND Optimus deliver massive revenue by 2028-2030. If you believe that probability is higher, TSLA is undervalued. If lower, it is a bubble.

### The Capex Efficiency Problem
Every other AI company spending at this level is generating meaningful AI revenue already. Their capex is EXPANDING proven businesses. Tesla's $25B capex is BUILDING businesses that do not yet exist. This is venture capital-level risk at mega-cap scale.

The counterargument: NVDA in 2016 was spending on AI with near-zero AI revenue. AMZN in 2006 was spending on AWS with near-zero cloud revenue. Every AI giant was pre-revenue once. The question is whether Tesla's AI bets are as structurally sound as those were.

ORACLE's assessment: Robotaxi is a real business if it works, but the regulatory and technical moats are less defensible than NVDA's chip architecture or AMZN's cloud infrastructure. Waymo (GOOGL), Cruise (GM), and Chinese competitors (Baidu, Pony.ai) are all pursuing the same TAM. Tesla's advantage is scale (millions of cars collecting data) but its disadvantage is approach (vision-only vs. lidar-assisted).

## ORACLE's Verdict

### Is TSLA an AI Stock?
**By spending: yes.** $25B in capex with 50-70% allocated to AI-adjacent businesses is an AI company's capital allocation.
**By revenue: no.** $0 in pure AI revenue makes it a car company that spends like an AI company.
**By market cap: partially.** ~$1T of the $1.3T market cap is AI option premium.

### What Should an Investor Do?
1. **If you own TSLA:** Hold. The EPS beat and margin expansion provide a floor. The AI optionality is real even if the timeline is uncertain. Do not sell on the $25B capex number -- that is the market's way of funding the pivot. But do not add above $420 without a concrete Robotaxi catalyst.

2. **If you don't own TSLA:** Do not buy at $400+ for the AI thesis alone. The optionality is already priced into the $1.3T market cap. If you want AI exposure, NVDA (proven revenue, 55x P/E) and AMZN (AWS AI accelerating, 40x P/E) offer better risk-adjusted returns with lower uncertainty.

3. **If you want to trade TSLA:** The AH reversal (popped 4% then gave it back) tells you the market is conflicted. Trade the range ($385-$420) until a new catalyst resolves the tension. Iron condor is the options play for this regime.

### The Dan Ives Question
Is Dan Ives right that Tesla is "more an AI company than a car company"? By aspiration and spending, yes. By revenue and proof, not yet. The market is paying $1T for the "not yet" to become "yes." That is either visionary or delusional, and only 2027-2028 will tell us which.
