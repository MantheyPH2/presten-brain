## Business Overview
XLE tracks the Energy Select Sector Index — all S&P 500 energy companies. Heavily concentrated in integrated oil majors (XOM + CVX = ~38% of the fund). Gives broad exposure to oil/gas exploration, production, refining, and equipment/services. Moves with crude oil prices but with corporate earnings leverage. This is THE sector rotation vehicle for energy.

## Why Track This
- **Sector rotation signal:** When money flows into XLE, it means institutions are rotating into energy — often a late-cycle or inflation trade
- **Oil price proxy:** Easier to trade than futures, with less contango risk than USO
- **Geopolitical barometer:** Iran tensions, OPEC decisions, and Russia sanctions all show up here first
- **Dividend yield:** Energy names pay real dividends — XLE yields ~3.4% which provides downside cushion
- **Correlation play:** Energy often moves inversely to tech — useful for portfolio hedging
