## Business Overview
XLF tracks the Financial Select Sector Index — all S&P 500 financial companies. Includes banks, insurance, capital markets, consumer finance, and fintech. Heavily influenced by interest rate environment, credit quality, and capital markets activity. This is where you see the economy's health in real-time — banks are the plumbing of the financial system.

## Why Track This
- **Interest rate proxy:** Financials benefit from higher rates (net interest margin expansion) — XLF direction tells you what the market thinks about Fed policy
- **Economic health signal:** Banks lend to businesses and consumers. When XLF leads, the economy is healthy. When it lags, credit stress is building
- **Earnings season barometer:** Big banks report first every quarter — XLF reaction in mid-January/April/July/October sets the tone for the entire earnings season
- **M&A and IPO cycle:** Goldman, Morgan Stanley, and JPMorgan benefit from deal flow. Rising XLF = more capital markets activity
- **Yield curve plays:** Steepening curve = bullish financials. Inverted curve = bearish
