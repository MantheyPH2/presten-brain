## Business Overview
Broadcom is a diversified semiconductor and infrastructure software company. Segments: Semiconductor Solutions (networking chips, custom AI ASICs, storage, wireless) and Infrastructure Software (VMware, CA Technologies, Symantec). The VMware acquisition ($69B, closed Oct 2023) transformed the business model — 40%+ of revenue is now recurring software.

## Bull Case
- Custom AI ASICs for hyperscalers (Google TPU, Meta MTIA) are a $100B+ opportunity
- VMware creates sticky, high-margin recurring revenue — not a commoditized business
- Networking dominance: if AI clusters need AVGO's switching chips, demand is locked in
- Hock Tan's M&A playbook is proven — every acquisition has created shareholder value
- At ~25x forward earnings, AVGO is cheaper than NVDA and growing nearly as fast on AI
- BofA top pick = institutional sponsorship at current levels

## Bear Case
- VMware integration risk — complex enterprise software transition
- Google and Meta building more custom silicon in-house over time, reducing AVGO dependency
- Stock already at $190-210 after big run — limited short-term upside vs risk
- Semiconductor cycle: if data center capex slows, AVGO networking chips feel it first
- High leverage from VMware acquisition (~$40B net debt)
