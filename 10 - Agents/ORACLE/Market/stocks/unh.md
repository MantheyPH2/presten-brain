## Business Overview
UnitedHealth is the largest health insurer in the US and the largest company in healthcare by revenue (~$400B annually). Two segments: UnitedHealthcare (insurance — covers ~50M people) and Optum (healthcare services, pharmacy benefit management, data analytics). Optum is the growth engine and higher-margin business. UNH is essentially a healthcare conglomerate that touches every part of the system — insurance, care delivery, pharmacy, and data.

## Why Track This
- **Healthcare sector anchor:** UNH is ~9% of XLV. As UNH goes, so goes healthcare
- **Earnings quality:** UNH has one of the most consistent earnings growth records in the S&P 500 — beat estimates almost every quarter for a decade
- **Optum growth story:** Optum is growing faster than the insurance business and has higher margins — this is the real value driver
- **Political risk bellwether:** Medicare Advantage, drug pricing reform, and insurance regulation all hit UNH first
- **Massive drawdown opportunity:** UNH has dropped ~35% from its highs — this kind of decline in a blue-chip compounder is rare

## ORACLE's Take
UNH down 35% from highs is one of the most interesting setups in the market. This is a company that NEVER drops like this — it has compounded 20%+ annually for over a decade. The Q1 2026 earnings beat and guidance raise on April 21 is a positive signal. At $325 and ~14x earnings, UNH is historically cheap. The risk is political — Medicare drug pricing and insurance regulation could compress margins. But the Optum growth story is intact and demographics (aging population) are a permanent tailwind. This is a HOLD leaning BUY. If it dips below $300, this becomes a strong BUY for a 12-month hold targeting $400+. The market is overpricing political risk and underpricing Optum's earnings power.
