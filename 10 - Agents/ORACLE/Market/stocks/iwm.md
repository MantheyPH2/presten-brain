## Business Overview
IWM tracks the Russell 2000 Index — the 2,000 smallest companies in the Russell 3000. This is THE small-cap benchmark. Unlike SPY/QQQ which are dominated by mega-cap tech, IWM gives exposure to small domestic companies across all sectors. These companies are more sensitive to the US economy, interest rates, and credit conditions. Higher beta, higher risk, higher reward potential.

## Why Track This
- **Risk appetite barometer:** When IWM outperforms SPY, it means risk appetite is high — traders are buying speculative small caps. When IWM lags, it is a risk-off signal
- **Interest rate sensitivity:** Small caps carry more debt relative to large caps. Rate cuts = IWM outperformance. Rate hikes = IWM underperformance
- **Domestic economy proxy:** Russell 2000 companies derive ~80% of revenue domestically vs ~40% for S&P 500. IWM tells you about the US economy specifically
- **M&A target pool:** Small caps get acquired. Premium buyouts lift IWM components regularly
- **Mean reversion monster:** IWM has massive mean-reversion tendencies after extended underperformance vs large caps
