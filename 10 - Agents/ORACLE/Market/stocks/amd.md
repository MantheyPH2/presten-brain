# AMD — Advanced Micro Devices, Inc.

## Current State
- **Price:** ~$108–$115 (estimate — exact not in data; update on market open)
- **Market Cap:** ~$175–$185B
- **Next Earnings:** Late April / Early May 2026

## Business Overview
AMD sells CPUs (EPYC server, Ryzen consumer), GPUs (Radeon consumer, Instinct MI300/MI350 data center), and embedded chips (Xilinx FPGA). The data center GPU business (MI300X/MI350) is AMD's attempt to crack NVDA's AI moat. EPYC server CPUs are taking meaningful share from Intel.

## Recent Developments
- MI300X gaining traction at cost-sensitive customers (Microsoft Azure, Meta) as lower-cost NVDA alternative
- MI350 chip announced — competing with NVDA H200 on memory bandwidth
- EPYC Genoa/Turin CPUs now at ~25% server CPU market share — Intel in secular decline
- AMD Ryzen AI CPUs for Copilot+ PCs launching across OEM partners
- BofA did NOT name AMD in its top chip picks (NVDA, AVGO, LRCX, KLAC, ADI, CDNS) — notable omission
- Tech sector's -9.3% Q1 performance hurt AMD disproportionately; stock down ~30% from ATH

## Bull Case
- MI300X/MI350 is the only credible alternative to NVDA for AI training/inference at scale
- EPYC server CPU is taking share from Intel every quarter — structural winner
- Export restrictions on NVDA H100/H200 to China means AMD Instinct chips can fill the gap
- At current valuation (~20-25x forward earnings), AMD is cheap relative to NVDA
- Lisa Su is one of the best CEOs in semiconductors — execution track record is excellent

## Bear Case
- NVDA's CUDA moat is 10+ years deep; switching costs are massive
- MI300X software ecosystem is immature — developers default to CUDA
- Hyperscalers are building custom chips (TPU, Trainium, Maia) — reducing AMD TAM
- BofA omission from top chip picks signals institutional preference is elsewhere
- AMD data center GPU revenue targets have been revised down multiple times
