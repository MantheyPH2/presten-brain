## Business Overview
Eli Lilly is the dominant force in the GLP-1/obesity drug revolution. Key products: Mounjaro (tirzepatide — diabetes), Zepbound (tirzepatide — obesity), Verzenio (breast cancer), Jardiance (diabetes), Taltz (autoimmune). The obesity/diabetes franchise is the growth engine — Lilly is competing with Novo Nordisk for dominance of the estimated $100B+ obesity drug market. Lilly also has a strong pipeline in Alzheimer's (donanemab), immunology, and oncology.

## Why Track This
- **GLP-1 revolution leader:** Lilly and Novo Nordisk are the two companies defining the biggest pharma trend in decades. Obesity drug TAM could exceed $150B by 2030
- **Pipeline optionality:** Donanemab (Alzheimer's), orforglipron (oral GLP-1), and retatrutide (triple agonist) each represent $10B+ revenue potential
- **Growth at scale:** 35% revenue growth on an $880B market cap is extraordinary. Very few companies this large grow this fast
- **Medicare coverage catalyst:** CMS decisions on Medicare coverage for obesity drugs are binary events for LLY
- **Pharma's NVDA:** LLY has become the "must own" pharma stock the way NVDA is the "must own" tech stock

## ORACLE's Take
LLY is the best growth story in pharma, period. The GLP-1 drugs are genuine blockbusters with Mounjaro/Zepbound revenue on track to exceed $30B annually. But at 55x earnings, the stock is priced for perfection. Any supply chain issue, competitive threat (Amgen, Pfizer GLP-1 entrants), or negative Medicare coverage decision would cause a 15-20% correction. At $927, this is a HOLD — the growth justifies a premium but not at any price. Buy aggressively on a pullback to $800 (200-day MA). The long-term thesis is intact: obesity is a $150B+ market and Lilly owns it. But do NOT chase this above $1,000 — let the chart come to you. Medicare coverage for obesity drugs is the next binary catalyst — bullish resolution sends LLY to $1,200+, bearish keeps it range-bound.
