## Business Overview
Goldman Sachs is the premier investment bank on Wall Street. Revenue mix: Global Banking & Markets (~65%), Asset & Wealth Management (~30%), Platform Solutions (~5%). Unlike JPM which is a universal bank, Goldman is more concentrated in capital markets, trading, and advisory. This makes GS higher-beta to market conditions — it outperforms in bull markets with high M&A/IPO activity and underperforms in risk-off environments.

## Why Track This
- **Capital markets thermometer:** GS earnings directly reflect M&A volume, IPO pipeline, and trading activity. Strong GS = strong capital markets
- **Higher beta financial:** GS moves more than JPM or BAC on financial sector rotations — better for trading
- **Trading desk dominance:** Goldman's FICC and Equities trading desks generate massive revenue during volatile markets
- **IPO and M&A cycle:** When the deal pipeline reopens, GS is the first to benefit
- **AI/Tech banking:** Goldman is the lead advisor on most major tech M&A — benefits from AI-driven deal activity
