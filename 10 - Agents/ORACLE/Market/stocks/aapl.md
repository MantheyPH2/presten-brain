## Business Overview
Apple's revenue mix: iPhone (~52%), Services (~23%), Mac (~8%), iPad (~7%), Wearables (~10%). Services (App Store, Apple Intelligence, Apple TV+, iCloud, Apple Pay) is the highest-margin segment and the growth story. iPhone 17 cycle in focus for 2H 2026.

## Recent Developments
- **MAJOR:** Tim Cook confirmed stepping down. John Ternus (SVP Hardware Engineering, led M-chip transition and Vision Pro) named as successor. Ternus is an engineer-first CEO — signals product focus over financial engineering.
- Transition is planned/orderly — this is not a crisis departure
- Apple Intelligence (on-device AI) gaining traction but monetization timeline unclear
- Services revenue growing ~14% YoY — most durable part of the business
- iPhone 17 expected to feature thinner form factor + enhanced AI chips
- $110B buyback program ongoing — float shrinking every quarter
- Stock has held $267 area despite tech sector's -9.3% Q1

## Bull Case
- Ternus as hardware CEO = renewed product innovation; Vision Pro 2, foldable iPhone
- Services at $100B+ annual run rate growing 14% — near-monopoly margin business
- iPhone installed base of 1.4B+ creates recurring upgrade and services revenue
- Buybacks reduce share count by ~3% annually — EPS grows even without revenue growth
- $267 is ~28x forward earnings — premium but justified for quality

## Bear Case
- CEO change introduces uncertainty; markets hate uncertainty
- Apple Intelligence has not driven meaningful iPhone upgrade acceleration yet
- China revenue (~17% of total) at risk from trade tensions + Huawei competition
- EU Digital Markets Act forcing App Store changes — services margin compression
- Ternus is untested as CEO; culture/strategy shift possible
- Stock near all-time highs with a leadership overhang — rotation risk
