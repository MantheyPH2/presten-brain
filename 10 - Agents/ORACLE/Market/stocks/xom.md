## Business Overview
Exxon Mobil is the largest publicly traded oil company in the world. Revenue segments: Upstream (exploration & production — ~60% of earnings), Downstream (refining & chemicals — ~25%), Chemical (~15%). The Pioneer Natural Resources acquisition (2024) added massive Permian Basin acreage, making Exxon the dominant Permian producer. Exxon is also investing in carbon capture, hydrogen, and lithium — hedging against energy transition.

## Why Track This
- **Oil price proxy:** XOM is the most liquid way to get oil exposure in equities. Moves with crude but with earnings leverage
- **Dividend anchor:** 3.3% yield with 40+ years of consecutive dividend increases. Dividend aristocrat
- **Permian dominance:** Post-Pioneer acquisition, Exxon controls the most productive acreage in the most prolific US oil basin
- **Energy policy barometer:** Drilling permits, LNG exports, and sanctions policy all directly impact XOM
- **Inflation hedge:** Energy stocks are natural inflation hedges — oil prices rise with inflation

## ORACLE's Take
XOM dropped ~5% on April 1 when Iran ceasefire talk eased geopolitical risk premium. At $149, it is in the middle of its range. The bull case is simple: OPEC keeps cutting, demand grows, Permian production ramps. The bear case: Iran deal floods market with supply, energy transition accelerates, oil demand peaks. At 13x earnings with a 3.3% yield, XOM is reasonably valued. This is a HOLD — own it for the dividend and inflation protection, not for capital gains. Buy below $130 for a high-conviction long-term hold. Sell covered calls above $165 for income. XOM is a portfolio anchor, not a momentum play.
