## Business Overview
Palantir builds AI-powered data analytics platforms for governments and enterprises. Three products: Gotham (defense/intelligence — CIA, DoD, NATO), Foundry (enterprise data integration), and AIP (Artificial Intelligence Platform — the growth engine). AIP allows non-technical users to deploy AI on their own data. Palantir is uniquely positioned at the intersection of AI, defense, and enterprise software. CEO Alex Karp is a polarizing but visionary leader.

## Why Track This
- **AI infrastructure play:** Unlike AI chip companies (NVDA), Palantir is an AI APPLICATION company — it turns AI into operational decisions for governments and enterprises
- **Defense spending tailwind:** Growing global defense budgets directly benefit Gotham. NATO expansion, Ukraine, Middle East — all drive Palantir contract wins
- **AIP is a game-changer:** The AI Platform is converting enterprise pilots into production deployments at unprecedented speed. This is the commercial growth engine
- **Rule of 40 monster:** A 127% Rule of 40 score (revenue growth + profit margin) puts PLTR in the top tier of all software companies globally
- **Cult stock dynamics:** PLTR has a passionate retail investor base that creates momentum on both sides — violent moves up AND down

## ORACLE's Take

But 70% revenue growth and a 127% Rule of 40 score are exceptional. The April 23 selloff (down 7%) on "sector pressure" creates an entry opportunity IF you believe in the long-term thesis. Buy on dips below $80 (200-day MA) for a long-term AI thesis play. If you trade this, respect the volatility — PLTR can move 10% in a single day. Use smaller position sizes.
