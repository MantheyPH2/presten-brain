## Business Overview
Chevron is the second-largest US integrated oil company. Revenue segments: Upstream (~75% of earnings), Downstream (~25%). Key assets include Permian Basin, Gulf of Mexico deepwater, LNG projects (Gorgon, Wheatstone in Australia), and Kazakhstan (Tengiz expansion). Chevron has a stronger balance sheet than most peers and returns more cash to shareholders via dividends and buybacks. The Hess acquisition (closed 2024) added Guyana exposure — one of the world's most prolific new oil provinces.

## Why Track This
- **Highest yield major:** CVX's ~4% yield is the highest among major integrated oils — this is the income play in energy
- **LNG exposure:** Chevron is a top-3 global LNG producer. As Europe and Asia shift to LNG, CVX benefits
- **Guyana optionality:** The Hess acquisition gave CVX a stake in Guyana's Stabroek block — one of the largest oil discoveries in decades
- **Capital discipline:** CVX maintains strict capital discipline — not overinvesting in high-cost projects
- **Buyback machine:** $75B buyback authorization — shrinking the float aggressively
