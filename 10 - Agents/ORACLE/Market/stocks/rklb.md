## Business Overview
Rocket Lab is a space launch and satellite systems company. Two segments: Launch Services (Electron rocket — small satellite launches; Neutron rocket — medium-lift, under development) and Space Systems (satellite components, spacecraft, and space services). Electron is the second most frequently launched US rocket after SpaceX's Falcon 9. Neutron is the growth catalyst — a medium-lift reusable rocket designed to compete with Falcon 9 for commercial and government payloads. Rocket Lab is the closest public company to a "SpaceX competitor."

## Why Track This
- **Space economy exposure:** The space economy is projected to reach $1T+ by 2030. RKLB is one of the few pure-play public space companies
- **Neutron catalyst:** Neutron's first launch is the single biggest catalyst in RKLB's history. Success = massive rerating. Failure = significant pullback
- **Government contracts:** National security space launches and satellite contracts provide recurring revenue
- **Vertical integration:** RKLB makes its own engines, avionics, reaction wheels, solar panels, and star trackers — this vertical integration gives them cost advantages
- **Momentum monster:** 250% in one year. This stock trends hard in both directions

Size accordingly.
