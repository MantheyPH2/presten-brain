### What Actually Happened -- ORACLE's Read

**1. EPS BEAT: $0.41 vs $0.35 -- THIS CHANGES THE NARRATIVE**
The Street expected $0.35. Tesla delivered $0.41. That is a 17% earnings beat. This is not a "lowered bar" story -- this is a genuine upside surprise. The bears who expected margin compression got the opposite: margins EXPANDED to 21.7% vs 17.7% expected. This is the best margin print in quarters.

**2. Margins at 21.7%: THE REAL STORY**
400 basis points above expectations. This means Tesla is either: (a) getting better pricing power than feared, (b) cutting costs more aggressively, or (c) benefiting from higher-margin software/FSD revenue in the mix. Whatever the source, 21.7% automotive gross margins demolish the "margin compression" bear case that has been the primary short thesis for 6 months.

**3. CapEx Guidance BALLOONED to $25B**
This is where it gets complicated. Prior guidance was ~$20B, already aggressive. Now it is $25B -- a 25% increase. This is the single most important number in the print for understanding what Tesla is becoming. $25B in capex puts Tesla in the same league as hyperscaler AI companies. The question is: where is this money going? (See TSLA AI Pivot Analysis for deep study.)

**4. The AH Reversal: Popped 4% then Gave It Back**
Classic TSLA. The initial reaction to the EPS/margin beat was +4% after hours. Then investors processed the $25B capex number and the delivery miss, and the stock round-tripped to flat. This tells you the market is conflicted: the earnings were good, but the spending is alarming.

**5. Dan Ives: "More an AI Company Than a Car Company"**
Wedbush's Dan Ives -- one of the most influential TSLA analysts -- declared Tesla is now "more an AI company than a car company." This is a narrative shift with real implications. If the market accepts this reframing, TSLA should trade on AI multiples (higher). If the market rejects it, $25B capex on a car company with missed deliveries is punishing.

The BULL read: EPS beat by 17%, margins expanded 400 bps above expectations, and the company is investing $25B to become an AI powerhouse. Dan Ives is right -- this is an AI company being valued as a car company. The upside is massive if Robotaxi and Optimus deliver.

The delivery and energy storage misses are real but overshadowed by the profitability beat.
