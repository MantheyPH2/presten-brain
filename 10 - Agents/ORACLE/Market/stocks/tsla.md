### What Matters Most — ORACLE's Read

**1. Deliveries: SOFT MISS, NOT CATASTROPHIC**
358,023 units vs 365,645 expected. The miss is 7,600 units — about 2%. This is a soft miss, not a disaster. More importantly, deliveries are UP 6.3% YoY, meaning the delivery trough is behind Tesla, not ahead. The market was bracing for worse given the Musk brand headwinds. This is neutral to slightly negative.

**2. Energy Storage: THIS IS THE BAD NUMBER**
8.8 GWh vs 12.45 GWh expected. A 29% miss on the one segment that was supposed to be the bright spot. Energy storage was THE bull case for non-auto Tesla bulls. Missing by this magnitude raises questions: is this a supply/production issue (fixable) or a demand issue (structural)? This is the number that will drive post-earnings selling pressure.

**3. CapEx Guidance >$20B: AGGRESSIVE**
Doubling CapEx to >$20B signals Tesla is investing aggressively — Megafactories, AI compute for FSD, Robotaxi infrastructure. In a vacuum, massive CapEx is bullish for long-term growth. But when you just MISSED on the segment that CapEx is supposed to be building (energy storage), the market will question ROI. This is a "spend more, deliver less" optic problem.

**4. EPS at Tesla's Own Lowered Guidance ($0.33)**
Tesla lowered its own guidance to $0.33 ahead of the print, below the Street's $0.36–$0.37. If they meet $0.33, they "beat" their own bar while missing the Street. This is the classic lowered-expectations management. Not impressive, but not a disaster.
