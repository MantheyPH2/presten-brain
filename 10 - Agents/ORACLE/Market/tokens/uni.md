## What It Does
Uniswap is the dominant decentralized exchange (DEX) and the inventor of the Automated Market Maker (AMM) model that powers most DeFi. Instead of order books, Uniswap uses liquidity pools where traders swap tokens against pooled reserves of two assets. Liquidity providers (LPs) earn fees (0.01% to 1% depending on pool) for supplying capital. Uniswap V3 introduced concentrated liquidity — allowing LPs to focus capital in specific price ranges for greater efficiency. Uniswap V4 launched in 2024 introducing "hooks" — programmable pool logic that allows custom fee structures, limit orders, TWAP oracles, and more. Uniswap processes billions in daily volume and is deployed across 10+ chains including Ethereum, Arbitrum, Polygon, Optimism, Base, and BNB Chain.

## Upcoming Catalysts
- **Fee Switch Implementation:** This is the single most important UNI catalyst. If UNI stakers receive protocol revenue, the token valuation fundamentally changes. Currently UNI is a governance token with no direct revenue claim.
- **Unichain:** Uniswap's own L2 chain (Unichain) launched — creates a new revenue stream for the Uniswap protocol and the UNI token.
- **V4 Hooks Ecosystem:** The hooks ecosystem is early — novel AMM designs emerging that could attract new use cases
- **Cross-Chain Expansion:** Uniswap expanding to new chains increases total fee generation

## Bull Case

- Fee switch is not live — UNI is still a governance-only token, which means the current price has limited value accrual justification
- Competition from Jupiter (Solana), 1inch, CoW Swap, and DEX aggregators eroding direct Uniswap market share
- If Unichain fails to attract meaningful liquidity, it's a distraction from the core product
- SEC has previously targeted Uniswap — regulatory risk for DeFi protocols remains
