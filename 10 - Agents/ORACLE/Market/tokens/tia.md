## What It Does
Celestia is a modular blockchain focused exclusively on data availability (DA). In the modular blockchain thesis, different functions (execution, settlement, consensus, data availability) are separated into specialized layers. Celestia handles only DA — it orders transactions and makes data available for anyone to download and verify, but does NOT execute transactions. This makes Celestia extremely efficient at its specific job. Rollups (L2s and L3s) post their transaction data to Celestia (instead of Ethereum) for a fraction of the cost, while still inheriting security guarantees. TIA is the native staking and fee token. The Celestia team includes members from the original Cosmos/Tendermint ecosystem.

## Bull Case
- The modular thesis is correct and growing — Celestia is the leading DA layer with first-mover advantage
- As more rollups launch, DA demand grows — TIA stakers earn fees from this activity
- Celestia's costs for DA are significantly cheaper than Ethereum blobs — real economic advantage
- The unlock overhang resolves over time — once distributed, the sell pressure diminishes
- TIA is a unique asset — pure DA play with no direct competitor

## Bear Case
- Ethereum EIP-4844 blobs provided better-than-expected DA at low cost — reduces Celestia's price advantage
- EigenDA and Avail are competing DA layers gaining mindshare
- The unlock is severe — 17.2% of supply hitting the market creates months of potential selling
- TIA's use case (DA) is infrastructure — not as narratively exciting as DeFi or AI tokens
- If the modular thesis loses to the monolithic chain thesis (SOL, SUI), Celestia's TAM shrinks dramatically
