## What It Does
XRP is the native token of the XRP Ledger (XRPL), a blockchain designed specifically for fast, cheap cross-border payments and asset settlement. Ripple Labs (the company) uses XRP as a bridge currency in its ODL (On-Demand Liquidity) product for financial institutions. The XRPL can settle transactions in 3–5 seconds with fees of fractions of a cent. It is not a general-purpose smart contract platform — it is purpose-built for payments and increasingly for tokenized real-world assets. The SEC's years-long lawsuit against Ripple Labs was finally dropped, removing a major cloud over the project.

## ORACLE's Take
The SEC lawsuit dismissal was a genuine fundamental change, not just a sentiment boost. Ripple can now openly work with US financial institutions, and the Rakuten/Kyobo announcements show they have been building a pipeline. The $55M ETF inflows are real institutional interest.

However, XRP has a complicated history with the crypto-native community — many see it as too centralized and too corporate. The token's value accrual mechanism is weaker than most L1s.

**ORACLE rates this HOLD. The SEC catalyst is largely priced in at $1.42. Wait for either a pullback to $1.20–$1.25 to accumulate, or an ETF approval catalyst to trade momentum.**
