### 1. Bitcoin (BTC)

**Est. Price:** ~$96,000-$98,000 | **Est. Market Cap:** ~$1.9 trillion | **ORACLE Rating: Buy**

Bitcoin is approaching the $100K psychological level — the most important price point in its history from a narrative perspective. The fundamentals are the strongest they've ever been: spot ETFs (IBIT, FBTC, ARKB) are pulling in billions in cumulative inflows, creating a persistent structural bid that didn't exist in prior cycles. Exchange reserves continue to decline. Long-term holder supply is near all-time highs. The April 2024 halving has reduced new issuance to 3.125 BTC per block, and miners are healthy — no capitulation signals.

The bull case is straightforward: institutional adoption via ETFs is a one-way door. Once pension funds, sovereign wealth funds, and corporate treasuries add BTC allocations, they don't remove them. The supply squeeze (halving + ETF demand + declining exchange reserves) is creating a structural imbalance that should drive price higher. Target: $120K-$150K this cycle, potentially higher if macro cooperates. The bear case: a macro recession triggers a risk-off selloff that drags BTC down 30-40% as leveraged positions unwind. Regulatory surprise (unlikely in the current US political environment). The $100K level becomes a sustained resistance that caps upside for months. Key catalyst: the $100K break itself — media coverage will trigger the next wave of retail FOMO.

### 2. Ethereum (ETH)

**Est. Price:** ~$4,300-$4,600 | **Est. Market Cap:** ~$520-550 billion | **ORACLE Rating: Strong Buy**

ETH is ORACLE's highest-conviction play in the current market. The ETH/BTC ratio is climbing, which historically precedes explosive ETH outperformance. The thesis has multiple legs: spot ETH ETFs are gaining traction (following BTC ETF adoption by ~12 months), staking yield (~4.2% APR) provides a real return that BTC can't match, and the L2 ecosystem (Arbitrum, Optimism, Base, zkSync) is driving transaction volume that settles on Ethereum L1 — generating fee revenue and ETH burn.

The Dencun upgrade (EIP-4844, proto-danksharding) has dramatically reduced L2 costs, accelerating adoption. The roadmap continues with full danksharding and further scalability improvements. DeFi, restaking (EigenLayer), and the emerging AI-agent economy are all building primarily on Ethereum and its L2s. Bull case: ETH breaks its 2021 ATH of ~$4,878 and runs to $6,000-$8,000 as institutional demand via ETFs accelerates and the "ETH is ultrasound money" narrative gains mainstream traction. Bear case: L2 fragmentation dilutes the value-accrual thesis. Solana or another L1 captures the marginal developer and user. The merge to PoS hasn't delivered the narrative impact expected — ETH still trades as a "tech stock" correlated to risk-on sentiment.

### 3. Solana (SOL)

**Est. Price:** ~$210-$225 | **Est. Market Cap:** ~$95-105 billion | **ORACLE Rating: Buy**

Solana has established itself as the primary alternative L1 to Ethereum in this cycle. The user experience is meaningfully better for retail — sub-second finality, negligible fees, and a mobile-first strategy (Saga phone, Solana Mobile Stack) that meets users where they are. The DeFi ecosystem (Jupiter, Raydium, Marinade) is thriving. NFT activity has been strong. The Firedancer validator client (developed by Jump Crypto) is a major technical catalyst — a second independent validator client dramatically improves network resilience and performance.

Bull case: SOL captures the "fast, cheap, good-enough" market segment. If crypto goes mainstream via consumer apps, Solana's UX advantage makes it the default chain. Firedancer delivery + continued ecosystem growth could push SOL to $300-$400. Bear case: the network has historically suffered outages, and while reliability has improved dramatically, another high-profile outage during a bull market could damage confidence. Solana's token unlock schedule has also been a source of sell pressure. SOL's validator economics and MEV dynamics are less decentralized than Ethereum. ORACLE's view: SOL is a strong hold but not the highest-conviction add at current prices — it needs to break $230 with volume to confirm the next leg up.

## Risk Warnings and Position Sizing Guidance

### Position Sizing Rules

1. **Never invest more than you can afford to lose entirely.** Crypto can draw down 70-90% in a bear market. Every dollar you allocate should be money you won't need for 3+ years.

2. **Core positions (BTC, ETH) can be 50-65% of your crypto portfolio.** These have the highest probability of surviving a bear market and recovering.

3. **No single altcoin should exceed 5% of your total crypto portfolio** unless you have extremely high conviction AND understand the specific risks.

4. **Memecoins and speculative tokens: max 5% total.** These are lottery tickets, not investments.

5. **DCA > Lump sum.** Spread your entry over 4-8 weeks. The volatility in crypto means a single entry point can be 20% from the local top.

6. **Set stop losses or hard mental exits.** For altcoins, a 30-40% drawdown from your entry is a reasonable stop loss. For BTC/ETH, wider stops (50-60%) are appropriate given higher volatility tolerance.

7. **Take profits on the way up.** Sell 20-25% of a position at 2x, another 20-25% at 3x. Let the rest ride with a trailing stop. Nobody went broke taking profits.
