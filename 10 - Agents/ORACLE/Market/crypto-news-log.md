# Crypto News Log — March 22 to April 22, 2026

> Maintained by ORACLE. All entries sourced from verified market intelligence. Dates are approximate — events are logged in the week they became market-relevant.

---

## Week of March 22, 2026

**Macro Environment**
- Bitcoin trading in the $82K–$88K range, holding above key support following a multi-week pullback from the Feb 2026 local high near $95K.
- Federal Reserve uncertainty driving risk-off sentiment across crypto. Markets pricing in fewer rate cuts than previously expected.
- Dollar strength (DXY) weighing on crypto as a risk asset class.

**Bitcoin**
- BTC dominance climbs toward 58% as altcoins bleed relative BTC value.
- Funding rates turn negative on Binance — the start of what will become a 46-day streak of negative funding. Historically, sustained negative funding precedes short squeezes as crowded short positions become vulnerable.
- Institutional BTC accumulation continues quietly via ETFs despite price softness.

**Ethereum**
- ETH continuing to underperform BTC. The narrative around ETH's relative weakness vs SOL and BTC intensifies.
- Developer ecosystem discussions around Pectra upgrade timeline.

**Solana**
- SOL maintaining $90–$100 range. Weekly dApp revenue surpasses ETH for the third straight week, a statistic that gains wider attention.
- SOL ETF discussions heating up following success of BTC and ETH spot ETFs.

**Regulatory**
- Post-SEC lawsuit drop against XRP, regulatory clarity narrative pushing XRP-adjacent assets.
- European MiCA implementation creating compliance costs but also legitimacy for major exchanges.

## Week of March 29, 2026

**Macro**
- Kevin Warsh emerges as frontrunner for Fed Chair nomination to replace Powell (term ending early 2026). Warsh is seen as hawkish — markets interpret potential Warsh nomination as negative for risk assets including crypto.
- Iran-US diplomatic tensions flare, adding geopolitical risk premium across markets.

**Bitcoin**
- BTC slides from $87K range to test $80K support. Warsh nomination fears cited as catalyst.
- Funding rates remain deeply negative — retail short interest building as price falls.
- On-chain data shows long-term holders not selling; coins continue moving from exchanges to cold storage.

**Altcoins**
- Broad altcoin drawdown accelerates. Most majors down 10–20% from March highs.
- ADA begins accumulation: whale wallets (424 tracked addresses) start accumulating, ultimately purchasing 819M ADA (~$214M) over the following weeks.
- LINK holds its range ($8.98–$9.33) better than most, showing relative strength.

**DeFi**
- Total DeFi TVL softens as price action drops underlying collateral values.
- PENDLE begins quiet accumulation ahead of a major protocol milestone.

**Deutsche Börse / Kraken**
- Deutsche Börse announces $200M investment in Kraken, marking one of the largest traditional finance → crypto infrastructure bets of the year. Signals TradFi's long-term commitment to crypto rails regardless of short-term price action.

## Week of April 5, 2026

**MAJOR EVENT: KelpDAO Exploit — $292M Hack**
- KelpDAO, a liquid restaking protocol, suffers a catastrophic exploit resulting in $292M in losses.
- The attack triggers a crisis of confidence in restaking protocols and DeFi security broadly.
- Aave, which had significant exposure to KelpDAO-adjacent collateral, sees $6.6B in outflows in 48 hours as users de-risk.
- Total DeFi TVL drops by $14B in the days following — the largest single-event DeFi outflow since 2022.
- AAVE token drops 16% in the aftermath.
- Security incidents cause DeFi liquidity to shift toward more battle-tested protocols.

**Broader Hack Context**
- Running total: $606M lost to exploits in the first 18 days of April alone — the worst month for crypto security since February 2025.
- The hack wave accelerates discussion around formal verification, audits, and insurance protocols.

**AI Token Rally**
- AI x Crypto tokens (TAO, RENDER, FET) post double-digit gains in early April, disconnected from broader market weakness.
- TAO consolidates around $240 support after a sharp rally. RENDER approaches $1.88–$1.92 resistance. FET rebounds from $0.14 support toward $0.20 resistance.
- Narrative: AI compute demand is exploding; decentralized AI infrastructure positioned as a hedge against centralized Big Tech AI dominance.

**Celestia (TIA)**
- April 1: 175.6M TIA tokens unlocked (17.2% of circulating supply). Classic supply shock event.
- Markets had partially priced this in, but the unlock still creates sell pressure as early investors distribute.

**Charles Schwab Announcement**
- Charles Schwab announces plans to launch crypto trading for its 37 million retail clients.
- Represents a landmark moment for crypto mainstream adoption — Schwab's client base is older, wealthier, and more conservative than typical crypto retail.

## Week of April 12, 2026

**Bitcoin**
- BTC continues grind lower toward $76K. The $80K level, which had been strong support, breaks with conviction.
- Warsh Fed nomination fears and Iran-US tensions cited as twin macro pressures.
- However: negative funding rates now at 46 consecutive days on Binance — the most extreme reading since early 2023, which preceded a massive short squeeze rally.
- Analyst community split: bears see structural breakdown; bulls point to negative funding + long-term holder behavior as contrarian buy signal.

**XRP**
- XRP rallies to $1.50 on renewed momentum post-SEC lawsuit dismissal.
- Rakuten (major Japanese e-commerce) and Kyobo (South Korean insurance giant) announce XRP-based payment/settlement partnerships.
- XRP ETF sees $55M in inflows in the week.
- Price pulls back 6% from $1.50 to ~$1.42 by week's end. Range: analyst consensus $1.15–$1.60.

**Solana**
- SOL ETF inflows surge — largest single-day inflow since mid-March on April 16.
- SOL surpasses ETH in weekly dApp revenue for the 5th consecutive week.
- BUT: Q1 2026 revenue fell 68% year-over-year. Developer count down 30% from peak.
- Narrative tension: strong protocol metrics vs deteriorating developer/revenue fundamentals.

**ARB (Arbitrum)**
- ARB rallies to 2-month high. Volume up 44% to $181M.
- Partnership announced with Eldorado, a LATAM gaming/payments platform with 1M+ users — significant for L2 real-world adoption narrative.

**SUI**
- CME announces SUI futures launching May 4 — a major legitimacy milestone (CME previously only listed BTC and ETH futures).
- $2.2M in inflows on April 20 alone.
- SUI price approaches $1.00 psychological level.

## Week of April 19, 2026

**Bitcoin — Current State**
- BTC: $75,901. Testing $76K resistance from below after a week of sideways action.
- Year-over-year: BTC is DOWN from ~$87K a year ago — notable because BTC ETF approval was supposed to be the catalyst for sustained higher prices.
- Dominance: 58.5% — highest since 2021, reflecting altcoin underperformance.
- Negative funding for 46 days: the market is historically oversold from a sentiment perspective.

**Meme Coin Sector**
- Meme coin market cap: $38B — down 75% from the $150B peak in November 2024.
- DOGE: $0.094, hugging the 50-day EMA at $0.095. No clear catalyst.
- SHIB: $0.000006076 — bleeding quietly.
- PEPE: $0.0000037–$0.0000039 range. Volume thin.
- WIF: no significant price action noted.
- The meme supercycle of 2024 is definitively over. Retail has largely exited.

**Cardano**
- Protocol 11 hard fork activates in April — the most significant network upgrade in Cardano's history since Vasil.
- $71M treasury fund approved by the community for Hydra (state channels) and Leios (parallel block production) scaling solutions — targeted for late 2026 deployment.
- 424 whale wallets have accumulated 819M ADA ($214M) over the past 4 weeks — significant smart money signal.

**DeFi Recovery Attempts**
- PENDLE surges 29.8% in 7 days — one of the standout performers amid the carnage.
- AAVE stabilizes around $91 after the post-hack selloff. $6B TVL remains but the $14B that left has not returned.
- $34M annualized revenue for both AAVE and PENDLE — actual protocol revenue giving them legitimate value floors.

**Macro Outlook**
- Warsh Fed nomination: if confirmed, markets expect a more hawkish Fed. Crypto typically performs poorly in rising rate environments.
- Iran-US tensions: geopolitical risk elevated. BTC has historically shown short-term correlation with risk-off moves, then decouples.
- The combination of macro headwinds + post-hack DeFi sentiment + altcoin exhaustion creates a challenging environment for the next 4–8 weeks.
