## The Landscape — What's Working on Fintwit Right Now

### Winning Content Types (Studied from Top Accounts)

**1. Data-Backed Contrarian Takes (Pentoshi Model)**
- Format: Single tweet with a specific number + contrarian interpretation
- Example of what works: "BTC funding rate has been negative for 47 days. The last time it hit 35 days, BTC rallied 45%. Nobody's talking about this."
- Why it works: specific data + "nobody is talking about this" = bookmark bait
- Our edge: ORACLE runs analysis 24/7. We have the data before most humans do.

**2. Earnings Preview/Recap Threads (10K Diver Model)**
- Format: 4-6 tweet thread breaking down what matters before/after a major earnings print
- Example of what works: "TSLA just reported. Here's the one number that matters — and it's not deliveries."
- Why it works: people search for these in real time. Twitter's algo surfaces earnings threads.
- Our edge: We update 11 stock trackers daily. We can generate earnings analysis faster and deeper than most accounts.

**3. Loss/Learning Posts (Transparency Model)**
- Format: "Here's where I was wrong and what I learned"
- Why it works: 95% of fintwit only posts wins. The 5% who share losses with clear lessons build the most trust.
- Our edge: Paper trading journal is designed for exactly this. When we start posting P&L, transparency IS the product.

**4. Annotated Chart Screenshots**
- Format: Screenshot with clear levels, arrows, annotations
- Why it works: visual, scannable, shareable. People screenshot and send to friends.
- Our edge: We track key levels for 30 tokens and 11 stocks. We can generate level maps rapidly.

**5. "Ranking" Posts**
- Format: "My conviction ranking for April 29 earnings: 1. AMZN, 2. META, 3. MSFT, 4. GOOGL"
- Why it works: rankings are inherently controversial. People reply to argue with the ranking.
- Our edge: We literally rank every asset with conviction scores. This is native to how ORACLE thinks.

## Voice Guidelines

**Do:**
- Lead with specific numbers, always
- Use "I" not "we" — it's a person, not a brand
- Admit uncertainty ("I don't know, but here's my framework")
- Be controversial with data backing ("TSLA's energy miss is worse than the delivery miss — here's why")
- Use clean formatting (bullets, arrows, numbers)
- End threads with a question to drive replies

**Don't:**
- Use emoji spam
- Post vague takes ("markets looking interesting today")
- Shill anything ever
- Post the same format every day — rotate between pillars
- Claim wins without showing losses
- Sound like a robot (ironic given ORACLE, but essential)
