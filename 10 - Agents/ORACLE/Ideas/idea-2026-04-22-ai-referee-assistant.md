# Idea: AI Referee Training & Decision Assistant for Youth Sports

## The One-Liner

A mobile app that trains youth soccer referees using AI-analyzed game footage and real-time decision support — filling a massive, completely unserved gap in the youth sports officiating pipeline.

---

## The Problem

Youth soccer in the US has a **referee shortage crisis** that's getting worse every year. The AYSO, US Youth Soccer, and club leagues are chronically short of officials at every age level. Why?

1. **Training is purely theoretical** — new refs get a weekend classroom cert and are immediately thrown into games with no feedback mechanism
2. **No affordable video feedback** — refs can't review their own decisions; they have no idea if their offsides calls, card decisions, or positioning were correct
3. **Burnout from verbal abuse** — untrained refs make more mistakes, get more abuse from parents, quit faster
4. **No career progression path** — there's nothing between "rec league side ref" and "trying to go pro"; talented young officials fall through the gap

Meanwhile, every club coach is on Evo Draw, which means Evo Draw is already filming games. The footage exists. It's just not being used for officiating.

---

## The Solution

**RefIQ** — an AI-powered referee training and development platform.

**Core features:**
- Upload game footage (or connect to Evo Draw clips) → AI analyzes referee positioning, decision moments, and outcomes
- Get a post-game "Ref Score" with specific moments flagged: "Your position on this play gave you a poor angle — here's where to stand next time"
- Offsides decision replay: frame-by-frame AI analysis of borderline offsides calls with confidence scoring
- Foul classification training: AI identifies contact events and trains the ref on the Laws of the Game standard
- Pre-game warmup: quiz mode on LOTG (Laws of the Game) rules with scenario-based questions
- Career tracker: build a verified officiating record used for advancement through USSF license levels

**Phase 2 (B2B):**
- Sell to state referee associations for bulk training programs
- Integrate with assignors (the software that schedules refs to games) — a "RefIQ certified" badge becomes a scheduling preference signal
- License anonymized referee decision data to USSF for national standards research

---

## Why Now

- AI video analysis costs have collapsed — computer vision APIs that would have cost $50K/year in 2022 can now be run for under $500/month at this scale
- Evo Draw has already proven the club-level video distribution model — piggybacking on that distribution is a natural path
- The referee shortage is documented, quantitative, and a political priority for US Soccer (they've been publicly discussing it)
- Youth sports platforms are actively looking for retention tools — clubs that develop and retain refs become preferred partners

---

## Evo Draw Synergy

This is the most interesting angle. Evo Draw already:
- Films games from the same angles needed for referee analysis
- Has club relationships and trust
- Has a coach-facing mobile app

RefIQ could be a **module inside Evo Draw** or a **standalone app with a "Powered by Evo Draw footage" integration**. Either way, the distribution is already built.

Revenue model options:
- Standalone: $9.99/month individual referee subscription
- Club bundle: $199/year per club (covers all refs affiliated with that club)
- State association license: $5–15 per registered ref per year

**US Youth Soccer registers ~3 million active referees** in a typical year. Even 1% penetration at $10/month = $3.6M ARR.

---

## Risks

- Liability concerns: if an AI "validates" a bad call, who is responsible?
  - Mitigation: frame as training tool only, never as real-time game assistance
- USSF relationship: any tool that touches officiating standards needs to not antagonize the national body
  - Mitigation: partner with state associations first; let USSF come to you
- Video rights: who owns Evo Draw footage?
  - Mitigation: clubs own their footage; refs would upload clips they have consent to use
