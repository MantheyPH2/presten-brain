# Momentum Trading Strategy

## What It Is

Momentum trading is buying assets that are going up and selling assets that are going down, based on the principle that strong trends tend to continue. It's one of the few quantitative strategies with persistent outperformance across 100+ years of data.

The academic basis is strong: assets that have performed well over the last 3-12 months tend to continue outperforming. This is called the "momentum factor" and it has been documented across stocks, bonds, commodities, and crypto.

---

## Why Momentum Works

1. **Behavioral biases**: Investors under-react to new information initially, then over-react as FOMO kicks in
2. **Herding**: Once a trend starts, more participants pile in
3. **Institutional flow**: Large funds take time to build positions, creating sustained buying/selling pressure
4. **Information cascades**: Good earnings → analyst upgrades → fund buying → more retail buying → momentum continues

## Core Momentum Signals

### Price-Based Signals
- **Rate of Change (ROC)**: Percentage change over X periods. ROC > 0 = positive momentum.
- **Moving Average Crossovers**: 10 EMA crossing above 20 EMA = bullish momentum. 50 SMA crossing above 200 SMA (golden cross) = long-term bullish.
- **52-week high proximity**: Stocks within 5% of their 52-week high tend to continue outperforming.
- **Relative Strength**: Compare an asset's performance to its sector or benchmark. Outperformers tend to continue outperforming.

### Volume-Based Confirmation
- **Rising price + rising volume** = healthy momentum (enter/hold)
- **Rising price + declining volume** = weakening momentum (tighten stops/prepare to exit)
- **RVOL > 2x on breakout** = institutional participation = strong momentum signal

### Indicator-Based Signals
- **RSI > 50 and rising** = bullish momentum (NOT overbought -- common misconception)
- **MACD histogram expanding** = accelerating momentum
- **ADX > 25** = trend has enough strength to trade

## Entry Strategy

### The Breakout Entry
1. Price consolidates near a resistance level (base building)
2. Volume declines during consolidation (coiling)
3. Price breaks above resistance on 2x+ average volume
4. Enter on the breakout candle close or on the first pullback to the breakout level
5. Stop loss below the consolidation low

### The Pullback Entry (Higher Win Rate)
1. Strong trend established (higher highs, higher lows)
2. Price pulls back to a key level (20 EMA, VWAP, prior resistance now support)
3. Momentum indicator shows the pullback is shallow (RSI stays above 40 in uptrend)
4. Enter on a bullish reversal candle at the pullback level
5. Stop loss below the pullback low

### Relative Strength Entry
1. Scan for stocks/crypto outperforming their sector/benchmark over the last 1-3 months
2. Wait for a consolidation or pullback in the leader
3. Enter when it resumes its uptrend
4. Ride the continued outperformance

---

## Exit Strategy

### When to Take Profit
- First target: 1.5-2x your risk
- Scale out: Sell 1/3 at target 1, trail stop on the rest
- **Trailing stop**: 2 ATR below price, or below the 10/20 EMA
- When RSI shows bearish divergence (price new high, RSI lower high)
- When volume dies on advances

### When to Cut
- Price closes below the 20 EMA (for day/swing trades)
- Price closes below the 50 SMA (for position trades)
- Break of the last higher low
- Your stop loss gets hit -- no moving stops further away

**The 3-12 month momentum effect is the strongest and most well-documented.**

## Momentum in Crypto vs Stocks

### Stocks
- Momentum works well over 3-12 months
- Tends to reverse at 12+ months (mean reversion kicks in)
- Best on mid-cap and large-cap stocks with institutional following
- Sector momentum (rotating into hot sectors) adds edge

### Crypto
- Momentum is MORE pronounced in crypto but also MORE volatile
- Funding rates can signal when momentum is overcrowded
- On-chain metrics (exchange outflows, whale accumulation) confirm momentum
- Works on shorter timeframes due to 24/7 market and higher volatility
- **Warning**: Momentum reversals in crypto are violent. Tight stops are essential.

---

## Risk Management for Momentum

- **Position size**: 1-2% risk per trade maximum
- **Correlation risk**: Don't hold 5 momentum positions in the same sector
- **Momentum crash risk**: Momentum can reverse violently in market regime changes. The "momentum crash" of 2009 wiped out years of gains in weeks.
- **Mitigation**: Always have a stop. Never "hope" momentum continues.
