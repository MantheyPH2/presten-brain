# Elliott Wave Theory -- Simplified (Only What Works)

## What It Is

Elliott Wave Theory says markets move in repeating wave patterns driven by crowd psychology. Price alternates between impulsive phases (trending) and corrective phases (counter-trend).

Full Elliott Wave analysis is notoriously complex and subjective. This file strips it down to the parts that actually provide a tradeable edge.

---

## The Basic Structure

### Impulse Waves (5 waves, with the trend)
```
        5
       /
      /
  3  /
 / \/  4
/   
1\  /
  \/
   2
```

- **Wave 1**: Initial move. Most people don't notice it.
- **Wave 2**: Pullback. Retraces part of Wave 1. Often deep (50-78.6%).
- **Wave 3**: The strongest, longest wave. This is where the money is. Typically extends 1.618x Wave 1.
- **Wave 4**: Pullback. Typically shallower than Wave 2. Often retraces 38.2% of Wave 3.
- **Wave 5**: Final push. Often weaker than Wave 3 with divergences.

### Corrective Waves (3 waves, against the trend)
- **Wave A**: Initial counter-trend move
- **Wave B**: Bounce (often traps traders thinking the trend continues)
- **Wave C**: Final corrective push, often equals Wave A in length

---

## Three Unbreakable Rules

1. **Wave 2 NEVER retraces more than 100% of Wave 1** (if it does, your count is wrong)
2. **Wave 3 is NEVER the shortest** of waves 1, 3, and 5
3. **Wave 4 NEVER overlaps Wave 1's price territory** (in impulse waves)

If any rule is violated, your wave count is wrong. Start over.

## The Only Parts Worth Trading

### Trade #1: Wave 3 Entry (The Money Wave)
**Setup**: You've identified Wave 1 (initial trend move) and Wave 2 (pullback).
**Entry**: At the end of Wave 2 -- typically at the 61.8% Fibonacci retracement of Wave 1.
**Stop**: Below the start of Wave 1 (Wave 2 can't retrace 100%).
**Target**: 1.618x extension of Wave 1 from the Wave 2 low.

**Why it works**: Wave 3 is where institutions pile in. It's typically the longest and most powerful wave. The risk/reward is exceptional because your stop is tight (below Wave 1 start) and your target is large.

### Trade #2: Wave 5 Entry (The Extension Play)
**Setup**: Wave 3 completed, Wave 4 pullback is in progress.
**Entry**: At the end of Wave 4 -- typically at the 38.2% Fibonacci retracement of Wave 3.
**Stop**: Below the start of Wave 4 (can't overlap Wave 1).
**Target**: Usually equal to Wave 1 in length, projected from Wave 4 low.

**Caution**: Wave 5 is often the weakest wave. Watch for divergences (price makes new high but RSI/volume don't). Be quicker to take profit.

### Trade #3: Wave C Completion (Counter-Trend Reversal)
**Setup**: An ABC correction appears to be completing.
**Entry**: At the end of Wave C (often at a Fibonacci extension of Wave A).
**Stop**: Beyond the Wave C target.
**Target**: Resumption of the larger trend.

**Higher risk**: Counter-trend trading, but the reversal can be powerful.

## What to Ignore

- **Complex corrective patterns** (triangles, flats, zigzags, WXY) -- too subjective, too many possible interpretations. If the correction is complex, just wait for it to end and trade the next impulse.
- **Sub-wave labeling** on timeframes below 1H -- noise drowns out the signal.
- **Forced wave counts** -- if you have to force it, it isn't there.
- **Wave counting as your primary analysis** -- use it as context, not as your sole entry reason.

---

## Practical Tips

- **Start with higher timeframes** (Weekly/Daily) to identify the big picture wave count
- **Use Fibonacci tools** to project wave targets and reversals
- **Combine with volume** -- Wave 3 should have the highest volume, Wave 5 often shows divergence
- **Don't over-count** -- if the structure isn't obvious, it's not a clear Elliott Wave setup
- **Multiple valid counts often exist** -- have a primary count and an alternative. If the alternative triggers, switch.
