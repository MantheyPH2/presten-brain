# The Top 20 Ways Traders Blow Up

## Capital Destruction Mistakes

### 1. No Stop Loss
**How they die**: One trade goes against them. They hold. It keeps going. What was a 5% loss becomes 50%.
**Prevention**: Every trade has a stop loss before entry. Non-negotiable. Hardware encoded into your trading DNA.

### 2. Averaging Down on a Losing Position
**How they die**: Stock drops 10%. They buy more. "Lower cost basis!" Stock drops another 20%. They buy more. Stock drops 50%. Account destroyed. They now own a huge position in a crashing asset.
**Prevention**: Never add to a losing position. If you want to add, only add to WINNERS (pyramid up, not down).

### 3. Position Too Large
**How they die**: Put 30% of account in one trade. Trade goes 15% against them. Account down 4.5%. That's manageable. But then they do it again. And again. Three bad trades = 13.5% drawdown from position sizing alone.
**Prevention**: 1% risk per trade. Maximum 5% of portfolio in a single position.

### 4. Using Excessive Leverage
**How they die**: 10x leverage. 10% move against them = 100% account loss. In crypto, 10% moves happen before lunch.
**Prevention**: Stocks: no leverage or 2x max. Crypto: 3-5x max. Futures: account for the built-in leverage.

### 5. Not Understanding the Product
**How they die**: Sell naked calls on NVDA before earnings. Stock gaps up 15%. Loss is theoretically unlimited. Account obliterated.
**Prevention**: Paper trade every strategy for at least 1 month. Understand max loss before entering.

## Strategy Mistakes

### 6. No Trading Plan
**How they die**: Make it up as they go. No defined entries, exits, or risk rules. Every trade is an improvisation. Results: random.
**Prevention**: Write down your strategy. Entry criteria, exit criteria, position sizing, stop placement. If it's not written, it doesn't exist.

### 7. Strategy Hopping
**How they die**: Try momentum for 2 weeks. Lose a few trades. Switch to mean reversion. Lose a few trades. Switch to options selling. Lose a few. Never develop mastery in anything.
**Prevention**: Commit to ONE strategy for at least 3 months and 100 trades before evaluating. Every strategy has losing streaks.

### 8. Backtesting Without Forward Testing
**How they die**: Backtest shows 80% win rate and 500% returns. Go live. Get destroyed. The backtest was overfit to historical data.
**Prevention**: After backtesting, forward-test with paper trading for 1-3 months. If forward results are within 70% of backtest results, the strategy is robust.

### 9. Ignoring Market Regime
**How they die**: Use a momentum strategy in a choppy market. Use a mean reversion strategy in a trending market. The strategy works, but not in this environment.
**Prevention**: Before trading, assess the market regime. Trending? Ranging? Volatile? Match your strategy to the regime.

### 10. Trading Too Many Markets/Assets
**How they die**: Watch 50 stocks, 20 crypto pairs, forex, and commodities. Miss the best setups because attention is spread too thin.
**Prevention**: Focus on 5-10 instruments maximum. Know them deeply. Better to be an expert in 5 names than a tourist in 50.

## Psychological Mistakes

### 11. Revenge Trading
**How they die**: Lose $500. Immediately enter another trade with double size to "win it back." Lose $1,000. Repeat until account is gone.
**Prevention**: Daily loss limit (2%). Hit it = done for the day. 15-minute mandatory cooling period after any loss.

### 12. FOMO Chasing
**How they die**: Stock is up 30% today. Jump in. It reverses 15% the next day. They are the exit liquidity.
**Prevention**: If you didn't plan the trade, don't take the trade. There will always be another setup.

### 13. Not Journaling
**How they die**: Make the same mistakes over and over because they never identify patterns in their failures.
**Prevention**: Log every trade. Emotional state, setup quality, rule adherence. Review weekly. This is how you evolve.

### 14. Trading When Emotional
**How they die**: Fight with partner, stressful day at work, didn't sleep well. Enter the market angry/tired/distracted. Make terrible decisions.
**Prevention**: Emotional check before trading. If you're not in a neutral state, don't trade.

### 15. Overtrading
**How they die**: Take 20 trades a day when 2-3 were legitimate setups. Commissions and bad entries eat the account.
**Prevention**: Maximum trade count per day. Quality over quantity. Boredom is not a reason to trade.

## Knowledge Mistakes

### 16. Not Understanding Fees and Slippage
**How they die**: Strategy shows 10% annual return in backtesting. After commissions ($5/trade x 500 trades = $2,500) and slippage ($1 per trade x 500 = $500), the real return is 4%. Tax takes another 1.5%. Net: 2.5%. Not worth the risk.
**Prevention**: Always account for commissions, slippage, and taxes in your P&L calculations.

### 17. Confusing a Bull Market with Skill
**How they die**: Make 100% return in a raging bull market where the index went up 50%. Think they're a genius. Market turns. Give it all back plus more.
**Prevention**: Compare your returns to a benchmark. If the market went up 30% and you went up 35%, your edge is 5%, not 35%. Humility saves accounts.

### 18. Not Diversifying Across Strategies
**How they die**: All-in on one approach. When that approach stops working (all strategies have drawdown periods), they're helpless.
**Prevention**: Have at least 2 complementary strategies (e.g., momentum + mean reversion). When one struggles, the other may thrive.

### 19. Ignoring Macro Context
**How they die**: Buy aggressively right before the Fed hikes rates into a recession. The setup was technically perfect. But the tide was going out.
**Prevention**: Check the macro environment. Fed policy, yield curve, VIX, credit spreads. Don't fight the macro.

### 20. Unrealistic Expectations
**How they die**: Expect to turn $5,000 into $1,000,000 in a year. Take excessive risks to achieve impossible goals. Account destroyed by month 3.
**Prevention**: Realistic targets: 15-30% annual return for a skilled retail trader is excellent. 50%+ consistently puts you in the top 0.1%. Anything promising more is selling you something.

## The Pattern of Account Blowups

Almost every blowup follows the same arc:
1. **Initial success** (often in a bull market)
2. **Increasing confidence** (bigger positions, more leverage)
3. **The big loss** (one trade too many, one position too large)
4. **Revenge trading** (trying to recover the loss quickly)
5. **The spiral** (losses compound, discipline breaks down entirely)
6. **Account destruction** (quit trading or start over)

**The fix**: Rules and systems at every stage. Position sizing prevents #2. Stop losses prevent #3. Daily loss limits prevent #4. Drawdown scaling prevents #5.
