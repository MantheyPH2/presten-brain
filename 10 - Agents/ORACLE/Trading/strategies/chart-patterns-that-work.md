# Chart Patterns That Actually Work (With Statistics)

## The Reality

Chart patterns work between 50-89% of the time depending on the pattern, the market, and whether volume confirms the breakout. Most retail traders overestimate pattern reliability because they cherry-pick examples. Here are the patterns with actual statistical backing.

---

## Tier 1: High-Probability Patterns (70%+ success rate)

### Head and Shoulders (Top)
- **Success rate**: 81-89% (depending on study)
- **Average target hit**: -16% decline from neckline
- **Volume requirement**: Volume should decline across the formation, then spike on the neckline break
- **Key**: Wait for the neckline to BREAK with volume. Anticipating the break kills win rate.
- **With MACD confirmation**: 85%+ success rate
- **Best context**: After an extended uptrend, on daily+ timeframe

### Inverse Head and Shoulders (Bottom)
- **Success rate**: 89% (some studies report even higher)
- **Average target**: Measured move from head to neckline, projected from breakout
- **Key**: The right shoulder should form on declining volume, breakout on high volume
- **This is statistically the most reliable reversal pattern in existence**

### Double Bottom
- **Success rate**: 78-88% (88% in bull markets with volume confirmation)
- **Average target**: Height of the pattern projected from breakout
- **Key**: The two bottoms don't need to be exactly the same price. Second bottom on lower volume is ideal.
- **Volume confirmation at breakout doubles the reliability**

### Ascending Triangle
- **Success rate**: 72-77% (breakout to the upside)
- **Why it works**: Flat resistance + rising lows = buyers getting more aggressive. Eventually, sellers at resistance are overwhelmed.
- **Average target**: Height of triangle projected from breakout
- **Key**: Must have at least 2 touches of the flat top and 2 rising lows. Volume should decline into the apex.

## Tier 2: Moderate-Probability Patterns (60-70% success rate)

### Double Top
- **Success rate**: 63-75%
- **Lower than double bottom because bearish patterns need more conviction**
- **With MACD confirmation**: 70%+ success rate
- **Key**: The two tops should be near the same price. Break of the neckline with volume confirms.

### Bull Flag
- **Success rate**: 65-70%
- **Average target**: The "flagpole" (prior move) projected from the flag breakout
- **Key**: The flag should drift DOWN on declining volume. Breakout on expanding volume.
- **Time**: Flag typically lasts 1-4 weeks. Longer = less reliable.

### Descending Triangle
- **Success rate**: 64% breakdown
- **Mirror of ascending triangle but slightly less reliable**
- **Key**: Flat bottom + lower highs. Breakdown on volume.

### Cup and Handle
- **Success rate**: 65%
- **Average target**: Depth of cup projected from handle breakout
- **Key**: The "cup" should be U-shaped (not V-shaped). Handle should be small and drift lower on low volume.
- **Time**: Typically forms over 7-65 weeks on daily charts

---

## Tier 3: Low-Reliability Patterns (Proceed With Caution)

### Rising/Falling Wedge
- **Success rate**: 55-60%
- **Too subjective** -- traders draw these differently
- **Can work but needs strong volume confirmation at breakout**

### Symmetrical Triangle
- **Success rate**: ~55% (essentially a coin flip on direction)
- **The problem**: Can break either way. Not useful for directional bias.
- **Only trade if you have a directional bias from other analysis**

### Pennants
- **Success rate**: 55-60%
- **Short-lived** (1-3 weeks). Quick move expected.
- **Best as continuation patterns in strong trends**

## Critical Success Factors (Applies to ALL Patterns)

### Volume Confirmation Is Everything
- Pattern formation on declining volume = building pressure
- Breakout on expanding volume (2x+ average) = real
- Breakout on low volume = likely false breakout (fade it)
- **Adding volume confirmation increases success rate by 10-20% across all patterns**

### Timeframe Matters
- Daily and weekly patterns are far more reliable than intraday patterns
- 5-minute chart "head and shoulders" is basically noise
- Minimum timeframe for reliable patterns: 4H, ideally Daily+

### Context Matters
- Patterns at the end of a long trend (potential reversal) = higher reliability
- Patterns mid-trend (continuation) = moderate reliability
- Patterns in choppy/ranging markets = lower reliability

### False Breakouts Are Common
- 30-40% of initial breakouts fail and reverse
- Solution: Wait for a retest of the breakout level before entering
- Or: Enter on the breakout but with a tight stop just inside the pattern

## My Pattern Trading Rules

1. **Only trade Tier 1 patterns** unless you have strong confluence from other analysis
2. **Volume MUST confirm** the breakout -- no exceptions
3. **Wait for the retest** when possible (reduces win rate slightly but improves R:R)
4. **Daily timeframe minimum** for pattern identification
5. **Stop loss inside the pattern** -- if price re-enters the pattern, the trade is invalid
6. **Target = measured move** (height of pattern projected from breakout)
