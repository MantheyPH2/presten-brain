# Smart Money Concepts (SMC) / ICT Methodology

## What It Is

Smart Money Concepts (SMC) is a trading framework that focuses on understanding how institutional players (banks, hedge funds, pension funds) move markets. Instead of relying on lagging indicators, SMC reads the footprints left by "smart money" -- the entities with enough capital to actually move price.

ICT (Inner Circle Trader), created by Michael J. Huddleston, is the most well-known version of SMC methodology. They overlap significantly but ICT adds specific timing and execution frameworks.

---

## Core Philosophy

Markets are not random. Large institutions must accumulate and distribute positions over time because their order sizes are too large to fill at once. This creates identifiable patterns:
- They **accumulate** at lows (buying without moving price up too fast)
- They **distribute** at highs (selling without crashing price too fast)
- They **engineer liquidity** by pushing price into areas where retail stop losses cluster

Your job: identify what smart money is doing, and trade in the same direction.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Market Structure
The foundation of everything.
- **Uptrend**: Higher Highs (HH) and Higher Lows (HL)
- **Downtrend**: Lower Highs (LH) and Lower Lows (LL)
- **Break of Structure (BOS)**: Price breaks a previous swing high/low in the direction of the trend -- confirms continuation
- **Change of Character (CHoCH)**: Price breaks a key swing point AGAINST the trend -- signals potential reversal
- **Market Structure Shift (MSS)**: Stronger than CHoCH -- a definitive shift from bullish to bearish structure or vice versa

### 2. Order Blocks
Order blocks are the last candle (or cluster of candles) before a strong impulsive move. This is where institutions placed their orders.

**Bullish Order Block**: The last bearish candle before a strong bullish move. When price returns to this zone, it acts as demand.

**Bearish Order Block**: The last bullish candle before a strong bearish move. When price returns, it acts as supply.

**Rules for valid order blocks:**
- Must precede a strong impulsive move
- Must break structure (BOS or CHoCH)
- Best when unmitigated (price hasn't returned to the zone yet)
- Higher timeframe order blocks > lower timeframe

### 3. Fair Value Gaps (FVGs)
A three-candle pattern where the middle candle's body is significantly larger, leaving a gap between the wicks of candles 1 and 3. This gap represents an imbalance -- price moved so fast that not all orders were filled.

**Trading FVGs:**
- Price tends to return to fill (or partially fill) FVGs
- Enter when price retraces into the FVG
- Place stop loss below/above the FVG
- Target: next significant level or order block

### 4. Liquidity
Liquidity = clusters of stop losses and pending orders.

**Where liquidity sits:**
- Above recent swing highs (buy stops)
- Below recent swing lows (sell stops)
- Above/below equal highs and equal lows
- Above/below trendlines

**Liquidity Sweeps/Grabs**: Smart money deliberately pushes price beyond obvious levels to trigger stops and create liquidity for their own positions. The price quickly reverses after the sweep. This is one of the most powerful SMC concepts.

### 5. Premium and Discount Zones
Using Fibonacci (typically 50% level) of a price range:
- **Premium Zone** (above 50%): Where you look to sell
- **Discount Zone** (below 50%): Where you look to buy

### 6. Kill Zones (ICT-specific)
High-probability trading windows when institutional volume peaks:
- **London Kill Zone**: 2:00 AM - 5:00 AM EST
- **New York Kill Zone**: 7:00 AM - 10:00 AM EST
- **London Close**: 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM EST

### 7. Inducements
Fake moves designed to trap retail traders. Smart money creates the illusion of a trend change, retail traders enter, then price reverses sharply. Inducements often appear as small breaks of recent structure that get immediately reversed.

## How to Trade SMC: Step-by-Step

### Setup Identification
1. **Determine higher timeframe bias** (Daily/4H) -- is the market bullish or bearish?
2. **Identify key levels** -- order blocks, FVGs, liquidity pools on HTF
3. **Wait for price to reach a key level** on HTF
4. **Drop to lower timeframe** (15M/5M) for precision entry
5. **Look for confirmation** -- CHoCH/BOS on LTF at the HTF level
6. **Enter at the order block** or FVG on the LTF
7. **Stop loss**: Below/above the order block
8. **Take profit**: Next HTF level or liquidity pool

### Example Trade (Bullish)
1. Daily chart shows bullish structure (HH, HL)
2. Price pulls back to a daily demand zone / order block
3. On 15-minute chart, price sweeps a low (liquidity grab) then forms a CHoCH to the upside
4. Enter long at the 15M bullish order block
5. Stop loss below the liquidity sweep low
6. Target: next daily supply zone

## When It Works
- Trending markets with clear structure
- During kill zones when institutional volume is present
- On forex pairs and indices (where institutional flow is dominant)
- When higher and lower timeframe analysis align (confluence)

## When It Fails
- Choppy/ranging markets with no clear structure
- During low-volume sessions (Asian session for forex)
- During major news events (structure gets destroyed)
- When you force trades without proper HTF-LTF alignment
- Overcomplicating with too many concepts at once

## Common Mistakes
- Marking every candle as an order block (be selective)
- Trading against the higher timeframe trend
- Not waiting for confirmation (entering too early)
- Confusing minor structure breaks with real CHoCH/MSS
- Ignoring the "why" -- SMC is about understanding institutional intent, not just labeling patterns
