# Swing Trading Playbook

## Why Swing Trading

Statistics favor swing traders over day traders:
- Cambridge University study: Day traders averaged -3.8% annually. Swing traders averaged +2.1%.
- 10% of swing traders achieve 10-30% annual returns
- Lower transaction costs (fewer trades = less slippage, commissions, spread costs)
- Better work/life balance (no need to stare at screens all day)
- Swing trades captured 68% of the S&P 500's total return over the last decade

**Holding period**: 2-10 days typically, sometimes up to 4 weeks.

## Setup Identification

### The A+ Swing Trade Checklist
Every swing trade should score at least 4/5 on this checklist:

1. **Higher Timeframe Trend Alignment**: Trading in the direction of the Weekly/Daily trend
2. **Key Level**: Price is at a significant support/resistance, order block, or supply/demand zone
3. **Pattern/Structure**: Clean price action setup (flag, base breakout, pullback to structure)
4. **Volume Confirmation**: Volume supports the thesis (declining on pullback, expanding on entry candle)
5. **Catalyst or Momentum**: Either a fundamental catalyst or strong relative strength vs. benchmark

## Core Swing Setups

### 1. Pullback to Structure
**The bread and butter of swing trading.**
- Strong trend in place (higher highs, higher lows on daily)
- Price pulls back to a key level (20/50 EMA, prior resistance now support, demand zone, Fib 50-61.8%)
- Reversal candle at the level (bullish engulfing, hammer)
- Enter on the close of the reversal candle
- Stop below the pullback low
- Target: prior high or Fib extension

### 2. Base Breakout
- Stock consolidates in a tight range for 2-4 weeks (forming a base)
- Volume declines during consolidation
- Breakout above the range on 2x+ volume
- Enter on breakout or on the first pullback to the breakout level
- Stop below the base
- Target: Height of base projected from breakout

### 3. Reversal at Major Support/Demand
- Price reaches a major weekly/daily support zone or demand zone
- Oversold conditions (RSI < 30, price 2+ SD from mean)
- Volume spike at support (potential capitulation)
- Reversal candle (pin bar, bullish engulfing)
- Enter with tight stop below support
- Target: Next resistance zone

### 4. Earnings Gap & Go
- Stock gaps up on earnings on massive volume
- Positive reaction = institutional buying
- Wait for the first pullback after the gap (usually 1-3 days)
- Enter on the pullback with stop below the gap fill level
- Target: Next major resistance or measured move

### 5. Sector Rotation Play
- Identify a sector showing relative strength (outperforming SPY)
- Find the strongest stock in that sector
- Wait for a pullback setup in that stock
- Ride the sector rotation wave

## Risk Management

### Position Sizing
- Risk 1% of account per swing trade
- Maximum 5 open swing positions (5% total risk)
- Calculate position size: (Account * 1%) / (Entry - Stop Loss) = shares

### Stop Loss Placement
- Below the pullback low / below the structure level
- Below the demand zone / order block
- **Never use a fixed dollar stop**. Always base it on the chart structure.
- Typical swing trade stop: 3-7% from entry

### Profit Taking
- **Method 1: Fixed R multiple**: Take full profit at 2R or 3R
- **Method 2: Scaled exit**: 1/3 at 2R, 1/3 at 3R, trail the rest
- **Method 3: Structure-based**: Exit at the next supply zone or resistance level
- Move stop to breakeven after 1R profit is reached

## Weekly Routine

### Weekend (30-60 min)
1. Review weekly charts of major indices (SPY, QQQ, IWM, BTC)
2. Determine market regime (trending, ranging, volatile)
3. Scan for swing setups forming (watchlist 5-10 names)
4. Review open positions -- adjust stops, plan exits

### Daily (15-30 min)
1. Check overnight news/gaps
2. Review daily candles on watchlist
3. Set limit orders / alerts for entries
4. Manage open positions (move stops if appropriate)

### No need to watch intraday unless entering/exiting.

## What Market Conditions Favor Swing Trading

| Condition | Swing Trading Edge |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Trending market (VIX 12-20) | Best environment. Clean pullbacks, clear trends |
| Low volatility (VIX < 15) | Good for base breakouts |
| High volatility (VIX > 25) | Reduce position size. Wider stops needed. Still tradeable. |
| Choppy/ranging | Harder. Reduce frequency. Focus on reversals at range extremes. |
| Earnings season | Great for gap-and-go setups. Avoid holding through earnings. |
