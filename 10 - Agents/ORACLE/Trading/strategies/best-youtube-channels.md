# Best YouTube Channels for Trading Education (Ranked)

## Ranking Criteria
Real strategy taught with evidence, no hype, no "secret indicator" garbage, no selling courses as the primary business model. Channels that make you a better trader, not a better customer.

---

## Tier 1: Genuinely Educational (Must-Watch)

### 1. Rayner Teo
**Focus**: Price action, strategy, risk management
**Why #1**: The most consistently useful, no-hype trading education on YouTube. Clear explanations. Applicable across all markets and timeframes. Focuses on foundational skills that actually make you money -- not flashy indicators.
**Best for**: All levels. Start here.

### 2. The Trading Channel (Steven Hart)
**Focus**: Forex, price action, SMC concepts
**Subscribers**: 2.55M+
**Why**: 100+ free videos teaching structured methodology. Clear, step-by-step tutorials. No upsell pressure.
**Best for**: Forex-focused traders, SMC learners

### 3. Lewis Kelly
**Focus**: Smart Money Concepts, supply and demand, market structure
**Why**: Makes complex SMC/ICT ideas easy to understand. Mix of beginner tips and detailed strategies. Practical trade breakdowns.
**Best for**: SMC/ICT methodology learners

### 4. Warrior Trading (Ross Cameron)
**Focus**: Day trading, momentum trading, small-cap stocks
**Why**: Professional trader with transparent P&L. Engaging presentation. Good for understanding day trading realities.
**Best for**: Day trading beginners. Understand his results are exceptional and not typical.

## Tier 2: Solid Education (Worth Following)

### 5. Akil Stokes (Tier One Trading)

**Best for**: Reality check. Understanding what trading actually looks like.

### 8. Option Alpha (Kirk Du Plessis)
**Focus**: Options strategies, systematic selling
**Why**: Best free options education on YouTube. Research-backed approach. Systematic, not discretionary.
**Best for**: Options traders, income strategy learners

## Channels to AVOID

- Any channel that promises "guaranteed returns" or "never lose"
- Channels where every video is selling a course or signal service
- "Trading lifestyle" channels (lambos, watches, Dubai)
- Channels that only show winning trades
- Anyone who claims a "secret" indicator that beats the market

---

## How to Use YouTube for Trading Education

1. **Limit to 30-60 minutes per day.** Watching videos feels productive but isn't a substitute for practice and journaling.
2. **Take notes.** Pause videos. Write down key concepts. Test them on charts.
3. **Verify claims.** If someone shows a "90% win rate strategy," backtest it yourself. Most won't hold up.
4. **Focus on one teacher at a time.** Consuming 10 different methodologies simultaneously creates confusion, not education.
5. **Practice > watching.** 30 minutes of chart review is worth more than 2 hours of YouTube.
