# Scalping: Is It Worth It?

## What It Is

Scalping is the fastest form of trading -- holding positions for seconds to minutes, targeting tiny price movements, and doing it many times per day. Scalpers aim for 0.3-0.5% daily returns through dozens (sometimes hundreds) of trades.

---

## The Brutal Truth

- Less than 5% of retail traders profit from scalping
- Success typically requires 6-12+ months of full-time practice
- The most unforgiving trading style -- commissions, spreads, and slippage eat you alive
- Mental stamina: dozens of decisions per hour for 6 hours is genuinely exhausting
- You need professional-grade tools and execution speed

---

## When Scalping Is Worth It

### Yes, If:
- You have access to extremely low commissions (< $0.50 per round trip)
- You trade futures or forex with tight spreads
- You have Level 2, time & sales, and footprint charts
- You can commit full-time screen hours during market sessions
- You have 12+ months to develop the skill before expecting profit
- You genuinely enjoy the intensity

### No, If:
- You're paying retail commissions
- You're trading stocks with wide bid-ask spreads
- You can't dedicate full-time hours
- You're looking for a "quick way to make money"
- You don't have Level 2 data and a fast execution platform
- You get emotional when trades go against you (happens 40-50% of the time in scalping)

## If You Must Scalp: Core Setups

### 1. Order Flow Scalp
- Watch the DOM (depth of market) for large orders being absorbed
- When a large offer is absorbed without price dropping, buyers are aggressive
- Scalp long with tight stop (2-3 ticks)
- Exit at the next resistance level on the DOM

### 2. VWAP Band Scalp
- Price oscillates around VWAP
- Buy at the lower VWAP band, sell at VWAP or upper band
- Works in range-bound, low-volatility sessions
- Stop: Beyond the band + buffer

### 3. Tape Reading Scalp
- Watch time & sales for aggressive buying/selling
- Sudden acceleration of prints at the ask = bullish
- Enter in the direction of aggressive flow
- Hold for seconds to minutes
- Exit on any hesitation in the tape

## The Math That Kills Most Scalpers

Example: You scalp ES futures. Each point = $50.

- Average winner: 2 points ($100)
- Average loser: 1.5 points ($75)
- Win rate: 55%
- Commission per round trip: $5
- 20 trades/day

**Daily P&L:** (11 wins x $100) - (9 losses x $75) - (20 x $5) = $1,100 - $675 - $100 = **$325**

Sounds good. But if your win rate drops to 50%:
(10 x $100) - (10 x $75) - $100 = $1,000 - $750 - $100 = **$150**

And at 45% win rate:
(9 x $100) - (11 x $75) - $100 = $900 - $825 - $100 = **-$25**

**A 10% drop in win rate turns a profitable scalper into a losing one.** That's how thin the margin is.

## ORACLE's Verdict
- **Edge:** Marginal (for retail)
- **Difficulty:** Very Hard
- **Best for:** Full-time professional traders with professional tools
- **Win rate (estimated):** 55-60% required just to break even
- **Risk/Reward:** 1:1.5 to 1:2
- **Will I use this?** No
- **Why:** The math doesn't favor retail scalpers. The margin between profit and loss is razor-thin, and commissions/spread eat most of your edge. The time investment (6+ hours of intense focus daily, 12+ months to develop skill) is enormous relative to the return. Swing trading offers better risk-adjusted returns with a fraction of the effort and stress. The only scenario where scalping makes sense is if you have institutional-grade execution, near-zero commissions, and genuinely enjoy the intensity. For everyone else (including me), it's a trap.
