# Supply and Demand Zones

## What It Is

Supply and demand zones are price areas where significant buying (demand) or selling (supply) imbalances occurred, causing sharp moves away from those levels. When price returns to these zones, the imbalance often reasserts itself, creating high-probability trade setups.

This is distinct from traditional support/resistance because zones are based on the cause of the move (institutional order flow), not just the level where price bounced.

---

## How Zones Form

### Demand Zone (Buying Pressure)
Price falls, pauses briefly in a consolidation, then explodes upward. The consolidation area is the demand zone -- this is where institutions were accumulating buy orders.

### Supply Zone (Selling Pressure)
Price rises, pauses briefly in a consolidation, then drops sharply. The consolidation area is the supply zone -- institutions were distributing sell orders.

**Key Principle:** The stronger and faster the move away from the zone, the more significant the zone. A slow grind away = weak zone. An explosive candle away = strong zone.

---

## What Makes a Zone Valid

### Strong Zone Characteristics
1. **Strong departure**: The move away was impulsive (large candles, minimal wicks)
2. **Fresh/untested**: Price hasn't returned to the zone since it formed
3. **Time spent in zone**: Minimal -- institutions don't want to show their hand
4. **Basing candles**: 1-3 small candles before the impulsive move
5. **Volume confirmation**: Higher volume on the departure move
6. **Higher timeframe alignment**: Zone aligns with HTF trend direction

### Weak Zone Characteristics
- Slow, grinding move away
- Zone has been tested multiple times (each test weakens it)
- Wide consolidation (many candles)
- Doesn't align with overall trend

---

## How to Draw Zones

### Step-by-Step
1. Identify a strong impulsive move (rally or drop)
2. Look LEFT to find where the move originated
3. Find the consolidation/basing area before the impulse
4. Draw the zone from the **high of the basing area** to the **low of the basing area**
5. Include the last candle body before the impulsive move
6. Extend the zone to the right

### Zone Types by Origin
- **Rally-Base-Rally (RBR)**: Demand zone in an uptrend (continuation)
- **Drop-Base-Rally (DBR)**: Demand zone after a reversal (stronger)
- **Drop-Base-Drop (DBD)**: Supply zone in a downtrend (continuation)
- **Rally-Base-Drop (RBD)**: Supply zone after a reversal (stronger)

**Reversal zones (DBR/RBD) are generally stronger than continuation zones (RBR/DBD).**

## Trading Strategies

### 1. Fresh Zone Strategy
Trade only the first touch of a freshly formed zone. First touch has the highest probability because all the institutional orders are still sitting there.

**Entry**: Place limit order at the edge of the zone
**Stop Loss**: Just beyond the opposite edge of the zone
**Take Profit**: Next opposing zone or key level

### 2. Flip Zone Strategy
When a supply zone is broken convincingly to the upside, it becomes a demand zone (and vice versa). This "flip" creates a powerful level because:
- It was resistance, now it's support
- Traders who were short are now trapped
- New buyers step in at this level

### 3. Trend-Following Zone Strategy
Only trade zones in the direction of the higher timeframe trend:
- **Uptrend**: Only trade demand zones (buy the dip)
- **Downtrend**: Only trade supply zones (sell the rally)
- Ignore counter-trend zones entirely

---

## Entry/Exit Rules

### Entry Methods

**Method 1: Set and Forget (Limit Order)**
- Place limit order at the proximal edge of the zone
- Set stop loss at distal edge + small buffer
- Set take profit at next opposing zone
- Walk away

*Pros*: Catches wicks, no screen time needed
*Cons*: No confirmation, may enter into a zone that's about to break

**Method 2: Price Action Confirmation**
- Wait for price to enter the zone
- Look for a reversal candle (pin bar, engulfing) on a lower timeframe
- Enter on the confirmation candle close
- Stop loss below the zone

*Pros*: Higher win rate, skips zones that break
*Cons*: May miss the entry if price barely touches the zone and reverses

### Risk Management
- Risk 1-2% of account per trade
- Stop loss always beyond the zone (not inside it)
- Minimum 1:2 risk/reward before entering
- If the zone has been tested 2+ times, skip it or reduce size

## Advanced Concepts

### Zone Quality Scoring
Rate each zone 1-5 based on:
- Strength of departure (1-5)
- Freshness (fresh = 5, tested once = 3, tested twice+ = 1)
- Time at level (quick base = 5, long consolidation = 1)
- Trend alignment (with trend = 5, counter-trend = 2)

Only trade zones scoring 15+ out of 20.

### Nested Zones
When a lower timeframe zone sits inside a higher timeframe zone, the confluence increases probability significantly. Example: A 1H demand zone that sits within a Daily demand zone.
