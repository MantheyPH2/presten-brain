# ORACLE Intraday Playbook

Every phase of the trading day has different characteristics. Trade differently in each one. This playbook covers equities (6:30 AM - 1:00 PM PT) with crypto overlay (24/7).

---

## Pre-Market (5:00 - 6:30 AM PT)

### What is Happening
Futures are trading. Pre-market orders are being placed. Overnight news is being digested. Earnings reports from before the bell are being priced. This is your preparation window — NOT your trading window.

### Actions
1. **Check overnight developments**
   - WebSearch: `"stock futures today premarket" site:cnbc.com`
   - WebSearch: `"premarket movers today" site:benzinga.com`
   - WebSearch: `"crypto overnight" site:coindesk.com`
   - Check BTC price — did crypto move overnight? Correlation check.

2. **Run the Pre-Market Scanner**
   - Identify gaps (stocks opening significantly higher/lower than yesterday's close)
   - Gap UP >3% = potential gap-and-go or gap fill setup
   - Gap DOWN >3% = potential oversold bounce or continuation lower
   - Log all gaps in the daily journal

3. **Review the HOT Watchlist**
   - Any overnight news on HOT names?
   - Any price alerts triggered?
   - Update entry/stop/target levels based on pre-market prices

4. **Plan the First 3 Trades of the Day**
   - Identify your top 3 setups from HOT list + scanner hits
   - Write the trade plan: entry, stop, target, position size
   - DO NOT place orders yet — wait for the opening drive

5. **Check the Economic Calendar**
   - WebSearch: `"economic calendar today" site:forexfactory.com`
   - Note any high-impact data releases (CPI, PPI, FOMC, jobs, GDP)
   - If major data release at 5:30 AM PT (8:30 AM ET): wait for the reaction before planning trades

### Key Rules
- **DO NOT trade pre-market** unless you are an experienced trader with a specific pre-market edge
- Pre-market volume is thin. Spreads are wide. Prices are unreliable.
- Use this time to PREPARE, not to TRADE

## Opening Drive (6:30 - 7:00 AM PT — First 30 Minutes)

### What is Happening
The most volatile 30 minutes of the day. Institutional orders are hitting. Retail orders from overnight queue are executing. Market makers are establishing positions. Prices are discovering their "real" level for the day.

### Key Rule: DO NOT TRADE THE FIRST 5 MINUTES
The first 5 minutes (6:30-6:35 AM PT) are pure noise. Spreads are widest. Fills are worst. You will get chopped up. Wait.

### Setups to Watch (6:35-7:00 AM)

#### 1. Opening Range Breakout (ORB)
- **Setup:** After the first 15 minutes (6:30-6:45 AM), identify the high and low of that range
- **Entry:** Buy when price breaks above the 15-min high with volume. Short when price breaks below the 15-min low with volume
- **Stop:** Opposite side of the opening range
- **Target:** 1.5x the opening range size, or next key level from tracker file
- **Best for:** Trending days (when futures are up/down >0.5% pre-market)
- **Avoid on:** Flat, low-volatility pre-market days

#### 2. Gap Fill Setup
- **Setup:** Stock gaps up/down significantly but starts reversing toward yesterday's close
- **Entry:** When gap fill is >50% complete and volume confirms the reversal
- **Stop:** Beyond the gap extreme (if gapped up and is filling, stop above the gap high)
- **Target:** Full gap fill (yesterday's close)
- **Best for:** Gaps on no news or low-quality catalysts
- **Avoid on:** Gaps on genuine fundamental catalysts (earnings beat, FDA approval)

#### 3. VWAP Test and Bounce
- **Setup:** Stock gaps up on earnings/news, pulls back to VWAP in first 15 minutes, then bounces with volume
- **Entry:** Buy on the VWAP bounce with volume confirmation
- **Stop:** Below VWAP by 0.5%
- **Target:** Retest of the opening high, then trail stop
- **Best for:** Strong earnings reactions where institutions are buying the dip
- **This is the BEST opening drive setup.** High win rate, clean risk/reward.

### Opening Drive Checklist
- [ ] Wait until 6:35 AM (skip first 5 minutes)
- [ ] Identify the 15-minute opening range on your HOT list names
- [ ] Note VWAP level on gapping stocks
- [ ] Execute only your top 1-2 setups (not all 3)
- [ ] Set stops IMMEDIATELY after entry — no exceptions
- [ ] If no clear setup by 7:00 AM, that is fine. Do nothing. The day is long.

## Mid-Morning (7:00 - 9:00 AM PT)

### What is Happening
The trend for the day is being established. If the opening drive was bullish, mid-morning typically continues higher. If bearish, it continues lower. This is the TRENDING phase — go with the flow.

### Setups to Watch

#### 1. Momentum Continuation
- **Setup:** Stock that broke out in the opening drive continues in the same direction with volume
- **Entry:** Buy the first pullback that holds above VWAP (for longs). First bounce that fails at VWAP (for shorts)
- **Stop:** Below the pullback low (longs) or above the bounce high (shorts)
- **Target:** Extension levels (1.618 Fibonacci from the morning range) or next resistance from tracker file
- **Best for:** Trending days with high breadth (most stocks moving in the same direction)

#### 2. Breakout Entry on Scanner Hits
- **Setup:** A stock from the pre-market scanner is consolidating at resistance and breaking out
- **Entry:** Buy the breakout above resistance with volume >1.5x average
- **Stop:** Below the consolidation range
- **Target:** Next resistance level from tracker file, or measured move (height of consolidation)
- **Best for:** Stocks on the HOT watchlist with confirmed catalysts

#### 3. Sector Rotation Follow-Through
- **Setup:** Sector ETFs (XLE, XLF, XLV, XLI) showing relative strength or weakness
- **Entry:** Buy individual stocks in the leading sector. Short/avoid stocks in the lagging sector
- **Stop:** Below sector ETF support level
- **Target:** Individual stock targets from tracker files
- **How to identify:** Compare XLE, XLF, XLV, XLI, XLK performance at 7:30 AM. Which is leading? Which is lagging?

### Mid-Morning Rules
- **Follow the trend.** Do not fight the established direction.
- **Trail stops.** If your morning trades are working, trail stops to protect profits.
- **Add to winners.** If a position is working, consider adding on the first pullback. Never add to losers.
- **Maximum 3-5 open positions.** More than that = you are unfocused and over-leveraged.

## Lunch Chop (9:00 - 10:30 AM PT)

### What is Happening
Volume dies. Institutional traders go to lunch. Price action becomes choppy, random, and mean-reverting. This is the WORST time to trade. More money is lost during lunch chop than any other period.

### What to DO During Lunch
1. **Review open positions** — adjust stops, take partial profits on winners
2. **Update tracker files** — any key level changes based on morning action?
3. **Run the options scanner** — mid-day is good for identifying unusual options activity
4. **Study** — read one research report, review one past trade, learn one new concept
5. **Prepare afternoon trade plans** — identify power hour setups
6. **Eat actual lunch** — seriously, take care of yourself

### What NOT to DO During Lunch
- **DO NOT enter new positions** unless a 5/5 conviction setup appears (this is extremely rare during lunch)
- **DO NOT revenge trade** losses from the morning. Lunch chop will make it worse
- **DO NOT stare at the screen** watching every tick. Nothing is happening. Walk away
- **DO NOT trade low-volume names** — spreads widen during lunch. Fills are terrible

### The One Exception
If a major news event breaks during lunch (Fed emergency statement, geopolitical event, surprise earnings), the lunch chop rules are suspended. React to the news, but only on names in your coverage universe.

## Power Hour Setup (10:30 - 11:30 AM PT)

### What is Happening
Volume returns. Institutional traders come back from lunch and start positioning for the close. Mutual funds and ETFs need to rebalance. Market-on-close (MOC) order imbalances start building. This is the second-best trading window of the day (after the opening drive).

### Setups to Watch

#### 1. Mean Reversion
- **Setup:** Stock that overshot in the morning (ran too far too fast) starts reverting to VWAP or the morning's average price
- **Entry:** When the stock shows a reversal candle (hammer, engulfing) near the extreme and starts moving back toward VWAP
- **Stop:** Beyond the day's extreme by 0.3%
- **Target:** VWAP or the morning's range midpoint
- **Best for:** Days with strong morning trends that are losing steam. Check if volume is declining while price continues — this signals exhaustion

#### 2. Momentum Continuation (Power Hour Edition)
- **Setup:** The morning trend continues into the afternoon with renewed volume
- **Entry:** Buy the first pullback after 10:30 AM that holds above the morning's higher low
- **Stop:** Below the afternoon pullback low
- **Target:** New daily high (longs) or new daily low (shorts)
- **Best for:** Days with strong breadth and consistent trend. Check advancing vs declining stocks — if breadth is strong, trend continues

#### 3. Closing Range Breakout Anticipation
- **Setup:** Stock consolidating in a tight range during lunch is about to break as volume returns
- **Entry:** Buy the breakout above the lunch range with volume confirmation
- **Stop:** Below the lunch range
- **Target:** Measured move = lunch range height added to breakout point
- **Best for:** Stocks on the HOT list that had bullish mornings and consolidated during lunch

### Power Hour Rules
- **This is where you execute your afternoon trade plans** prepared during lunch
- **Mean reversion works best here** — stocks that went too far in the morning often revert in the afternoon
- **Watch MOC order imbalances** — large buy imbalances = stocks going up into close. Large sell imbalances = stocks going down into close
- WebSearch: `"market on close imbalance" site:twitter.com` for real-time MOC data

## Last 30 Minutes (12:30 - 1:00 PM PT)

### What is Happening
The final push. Market-on-close (MOC) orders are hitting. Mutual funds are executing rebalance trades. Day traders are closing positions. Volume is typically the second-highest of the day (after the opening). This period can be violent in either direction.

### Actions

#### If You Have Winning Day Trades
- **Take profit before 12:45 PM** on any position you do NOT want to hold overnight
- Trail stops tight — the last 15 minutes can reverse gains quickly
- Lock in at least partial profits (sell half, trail the rest)

#### If You Want to Hold Overnight
- **Adjust stops for overnight risk** — widen stops by 1.5x the normal distance to account for after-hours and pre-market gaps
- Check after-hours earnings calendar — is anyone reporting tonight who could gap your position?
- Reduce position size if holding overnight on a volatile name

#### If You Are Flat (No Positions)
- **Do NOT enter new day trades in the last 30 minutes** unless you plan to hold overnight
- Use this time to observe: which stocks are seeing MOC buy imbalances? These may gap up tomorrow
- Note the closing levels on your WARM watchlist names — are any approaching trigger levels?

### Crypto at Equity Close
- Crypto does not have a "close" but it is highly correlated with equities during market hours
- BTC often makes a directional move at 1:00 PM PT as equity correlation kicks in
- If equities close strong, BTC tends to continue higher in the next 1-2 hours
- If equities close weak, BTC often dips in the next 1-2 hours
- This is an edge: trade crypto's reaction to the equity close

### Last 30 Minutes Checklist
- [ ] Close all day trades you do not want to hold overnight
- [ ] Adjust stops on overnight positions
- [ ] Note MOC order imbalances for tomorrow's pre-market scan
- [ ] Record the day's final P&L
- [ ] Check after-hours earnings calendar
- [ ] Note the closing price on all HOT watchlist names
