## The Business Cycle Rotation Framework

The economy moves in cycles. Each phase favors different sectors. Identifying where we are = knowing what to buy and what to sell.

```
         EARLY RECOVERY          MID CYCLE            LATE CYCLE           RECESSION
         ──────────────          ─────────            ──────────           ─────────
         Consumer Disc.    →     Technology      →    Energy          →   Utilities
         Financials        →     Industrials     →    Healthcare      →   Healthcare
         Real Estate       →     Materials       →    Consumer Staples→   Consumer Staples
         Technology        →     Communication   →    Materials       →   Gold/Treasuries

GDP:     Accelerating            Strong                Slowing              Contracting
Rates:   Low/Falling             Rising                High/Peaking         Falling
Yield:   Steepening              Flattening            Inverting            Steepening
Fed:     Easing                  Neutral/Tightening    Tightening           Easing
Credit:  Expanding               Available             Tightening           Frozen
```

### How to Identify Where We Are NOW (April 2026)

**Current signals:**
- S&P 500 at record highs (~7,108) after Iran ceasefire rally — but pulling back on uncertainty
- VIX at ~19 — moderate, not complacent but not panicked
- Oil above $105/bbl — elevated, historically a late-cycle drag
- Value outperforming growth in 2026 — classic late-cycle rotation
- Small caps outpacing large caps — broadening participation
- Energy +25% YTD, Materials +17.9%, Staples +15.9% — defensive/commodity leadership
- Technology -3.6% YTD through February — lagging for first time in years
- Industrials +14.3% YTD — still participating
- Fed policy: rates easing but cautiously amid inflation from oil shock

**ORACLE's cycle assessment: LATE CYCLE with pockets of broadening**
- Real assets leading (energy, materials, gold) = classic late cycle
- Defensive sectors (staples, utilities) outperforming = money seeking safety
- Tech lagging = growth premium compressing
- But: earnings growth broadening to small caps = not yet recessionary
- Wild card: Iran war and oil above $100 could accelerate the cycle toward recession

## How to Trade Sector Rotation

### The Core Strategy
1. **Go LONG** the sector money is flowing INTO (relative strength breakout, volume surge)
2. **Go SHORT** (or avoid/underweight) the sector money is flowing OUT OF
3. **Use sector ETFs** (XLE, XLK, etc.) for broad, low-risk exposure
4. **Use top stocks** within the hot sector for concentrated, higher-conviction bets
5. **Rotate every 2-6 weeks** as leadership shifts — don't marry a sector

### Rotation Signals to Watch
| Signal | What It Means | Action |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| Sector ETF breaks above 50-day MA on volume | New uptrend starting | Initiate long position |
| Sector ETF breaks below 50-day MA on volume | Uptrend ending | Exit or reduce |
| Sector RS line making new highs vs SPY | Outperformance accelerating | Add to position |
| Sector RS line rolling over vs SPY | Leadership fading | Take profits |
| Earnings revision breadth turning positive | Fundamental improvement | Early rotation signal |
| Earnings revision breadth turning negative | Fundamental deterioration | Late rotation signal |
| Unusual sector ETF volume (3x+ average) | Big money repositioning | Investigate and follow |

### Relative Strength Calculation
```
RS Ratio = (Sector ETF Price / SPY Price) × 100

Plot over 20, 50, 200 days:
- RS rising + above 50-day MA = STRONG (overweight)
- RS falling + below 50-day MA = WEAK (underweight)
- RS crossing above 50-day MA = EMERGING LEADER (initiate)
- RS crossing below 50-day MA = FADING LEADER (reduce)
```

### Pair Trade Strategy
Go long the strongest sector, short the weakest sector. This is market-neutral and profits from the rotation itself.

**Current example (April 2026):**
- LONG XLE (strongest sector, +25% YTD)
- SHORT XLK (weakest sector, -3.6% YTD)
- This profits regardless of market direction — as long as the rotation continues

**Risk:** Rotation reversal. Use a stop if the RS ratio between the pair reverses 2+ standard deviations.

## Key Rules for Sector Rotation Trading

1. **The trend is your friend until it bends.** Don't fight the rotation. If energy is leading, own energy. Don't try to bottom-fish tech.
2. **Rotation takes 2-8 weeks to play out.** Don't expect overnight moves. Be patient.
3. **The first sector to break out of a range is usually the new leader.** Watch for volume confirmation.
4. **Sector rotation accelerates during earnings season.** Beat/miss rates within a sector confirm or deny the rotation thesis.
5. **When ALL sectors are falling together, it's not rotation — it's a bear market.** Raise cash instead.
6. **The reversal of rotation is the most profitable trade.** When tech starts outperforming after months of underperformance, the snapback is violent. Be ready.
