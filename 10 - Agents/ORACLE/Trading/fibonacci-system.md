# Fibonacci System

> A single Fibonacci level is a guess. Two aligned levels is a zone. Four aligned levels is a trade.

---

## Why Fibonacci Works

Fibonacci retracement works not because of mystical mathematics, but because enough traders watch the same levels that they become self-fulfilling. More importantly, Fibonacci levels align naturally with human psychology — pullbacks of "about half" or "about two-thirds" feel natural because buyers and sellers genuinely re-evaluate at those intervals.

**Research finding:** Fibonacci alone adds marginal edge (barely better than random). The edge comes from *confluence* — when Fibonacci aligns with other independent signals:

- Fibonacci alone: ~50% win rate (coin flip)
- + Volume confirmation: +12% win rate
- + Multi-timeframe confluence: +8% win rate
- + RSI divergence at the level: +15–20% win rate
- + Horizontal S/R alignment: meaningful additional edge

This is why ORACLE's rule is simple: **never trade a Fibonacci level in isolation.**

## The Core Fibonacci Levels

### Retracement Levels (entries)

| Level | Character | Best Use |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **23.6%** | Shallow retracement | Only found in extremely strong momentum trends; short trade window |
| **38.2%** | Normal pullback | Primary entry zone in strong uptrends; first target for bounce |
| **50.0%** | Psychological midpoint | Not a true Fibonacci number, but widely watched; strong psychological support |
| **61.8%** | The Golden Ratio | Deep pullback, trend still intact; most reliable level for reversals |
| **78.6%** | Last stand | Final chance for trend continuation before likely reversal |

**The 38.2% and 61.8% are ORACLE's two money levels.** The others are secondary unless confluence confirms them.

### Extension Levels (profit targets)

| Level | Character |
|-------|-----------|
| **1.272** | First extension; conservative target (take partial) |
| **1.618** | Golden extension; primary full target in most trades |
| **2.0** | Full extension; strong momentum only |
| **2.618** | Extreme extension; only in parabolic trends with no overhead resistance |

## How to Draw Fibonacci Correctly

**The single biggest mistake:** drawing from the wrong swing points. Fibonacci is only as useful as the pivots you draw from.

### Uptrend Retracement
1. Identify the most recent *major* swing low (where the trend started or most recently accelerated)
2. Identify the most recent *major* swing high (the peak before the pullback you're trading)
3. Draw from **low → high**
4. The retracement levels now show where pullbacks are expected to find support

### Downtrend Retracement
1. Identify the most recent *major* swing high
2. Identify the most recent *major* swing low (where the decline paused)
3. Draw from **high → low**
4. The retracement levels show where bounces are expected to find resistance

### Choosing the Right Swing Points

**Use major swings, not minor ones:**
- Major swing = price pivot visible on the timeframe you're trading + the next timeframe up
- Minor swing = noise on the lower timeframe only
- If in doubt: a valid swing is defined by at least 2–3 candles making lower highs on each side (for a swing high) or higher lows (for a swing low)

**Timeframe consistency:**
- Swing trade (days to weeks): draw on daily chart, confirm on 4H
- Intraday trade (hours): draw on 1H chart, confirm on 15min
- Scalp (minutes): draw on 15min, confirm on 5min
- Always draw from the HIGHER timeframe — it has more significance

## Fibonacci Confluence (Where the Real Edge Is)

Confluence means multiple independent signals aligning at the same price zone. Each additional layer raises the probability of a reaction.

**Conviction framework:**
- **1 layer** (fib level alone): Do not trade
- **2 layers**: Watch zone; small size if other criteria met
- **3 layers**: Tradeable with normal position sizing
- **4+ layers**: High conviction — can size up or use more aggressive options structure

### Types of Confluence to Stack

**Fib + Horizontal S/R:**
The most powerful. A level where price historically reversed multiple times *and* a Fibonacci level from an independent swing land at the same price. The zone has both mathematical and historical significance.

**Fib + Moving Average:**
- 50 SMA / EMA pulling up through a 38.2% or 61.8% retracement = strong confluence
- 200 SMA at a 50% or 61.8% retracement in a strong trend = very high conviction
- Moving average acts as dynamic support; fib level adds precision

**Fib + VWAP / Anchored VWAP:**
- Anchored VWAP from a key date (earnings, major low) acting as support exactly at a fib level
- VWAP is the institutional "fair value" anchor — when it aligns with fib, institutions are likely to defend that level

**Fib + Volume Profile:**
- Point of Control (POC) or high-volume node at a Fibonacci level
- Institutions traded heavily at this price; they're likely to defend it as support/resistance

**Fib + Trendline:**
- Ascending trendline intersecting at the 38.2% or 61.8% on the day you're watching
- Dynamic and static levels converging

**Fib + Another Fib (from different swings):**
This is the most underused confluence. Draw fibs from *two different swing lengths* — if the 61.8% of the shorter swing and the 38.2% of the longer swing land at the same price, that's a high-probability zone. The market has no reason to make those two levels coincide except structural significance.

**Fib + RSI Divergence:**
- Price touches 61.8% retracement AND RSI shows bullish divergence (higher low on RSI, lower low on price)
- Research shows this combination adds 15–20% to win rate

## Fibonacci Extensions: Setting Profit Targets

Once you're in a trade and price has confirmed support at a Fibonacci retracement level, use extensions to project targets.

**Drawing extensions:**
1. Use the same swing you drew the retracement from
2. The 1.0 level = the original high (breakeven above the swing)
3. 1.272, 1.618, 2.0 = projected extension targets above

**Practical targeting:**
- In most trades: take 50% of position at 1.272, let 50% run to 1.618
- In strong momentum: take first partial at 1.618, target 2.618 with the rest
- In weak trend or chop: don't let greed hold you past 1.272

**Fib extension + resistance:**
Before placing a 1.618 target, check whether there's major horizontal resistance before that level. If there is, target the resistance instead — the market doesn't care about your Fibonacci extension if there's a wall of sellers above it.

## Multi-Timeframe Fibonacci (Advanced)

The highest-probability setups occur when the *same price zone* is significant on multiple timeframes.

**Process:**
1. On the weekly chart: note the major retracement level (e.g., 61.8% of the 52-week move = $148)
2. On the daily chart: note the 38.2% of the recent rally (also lands at $148)
3. On the 4H chart: note a POC and 200 EMA converging at $148
4. That $148 zone is a multi-timeframe, multi-signal confluence — trade it with high conviction

**Research finding (2025):** Multi-timeframe Fibonacci confluence adds approximately 8% to base win rate versus single-timeframe fib alone.

## ORACLE's Fibonacci Trading Rules

1. **Never trade a single fib level alone** — minimum 3 layers of confluence to take a position
2. **The 38.2% and 61.8% are the money levels** — all else is secondary
3. **Fibonacci works in trends, not ranges** — if the market is ranging (no clear swing structure), do not apply Fibonacci; it will give false levels
4. **Draw from the CORRECT swing points** — major, clearly visible pivots on the trading timeframe and one timeframe up
5. **Always check what's beyond the entry level** — are there extension targets clear of resistance?
6. **Add RSI and volume analysis to every fib setup** — these two additions dramatically increase win rate
7. **Re-draw after every major new swing** — old Fibonacci levels become stale as new swings form
8. **Fib is an entry zone, not an exact price** — trade a zone of ±0.5–1% around the level, not a single tick

## Common Fibonacci Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Drawing from every minor swing | Creates noise, too many levels | Only major swings visible on trading TF + one TF up |
| Trading 50% fib in ranging market | 50% of a range is the middle — not meaningful | Apply fib only in trending, swing-defined environments |
| Using fib level as exact price | Gets stopped out by normal volatility at the level | Trade a zone (±0.5–1%) with confirmation candle |
| Ignoring what's above the extension target | Running into major resistance before 1.618 | Always map the path before entering |
| Over-drawing (every swing on the chart) | Too much information, analysis paralysis | One primary swing, one secondary swing max |
| Not waiting for confirmation at the level | Catching falling knives | Wait for a confirmation candle (hammer, engulfing) at the fib zone |
