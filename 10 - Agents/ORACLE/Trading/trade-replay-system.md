# Trade Replay System

> The trade ends when you exit. The learning begins when you replay. Every unreplayed loss is a loss you'll repeat.

---

## Why Trade Replay Exists

Most traders review their P&L. Very few review their *process*. A profitable trade executed poorly is dangerous — it reinforces bad behavior with a good outcome. A losing trade executed perfectly is valuable — it shows the process is sound even when results aren't.

The goal of trade replay is not to feel good or bad about a trade. It is to extract accurate information about what you're doing and why, so you can do more of what works and less of what doesn't.

**What trade replay is:**
- A structured, honest post-mortem of every significant trade
- A record of decisions, not just outcomes
- The single most powerful tool for accelerating skill development

**What trade replay is not:**
- Hindsight criticism ("I should have known the stock would drop")
- P&L obsession
- Optional

---

## When to Replay a Trade

| Situation | Replay Required? |
|-----------|----------------|
| Any losing trade | Yes — mandatory |
| Any trade graded C or F | Yes — mandatory |
| Best winning trade of the week | Yes — learn from success too |
| Any trade where you felt emotional (fear, greed, revenge) during execution | Yes — mandatory |
| Clean, by-the-rules winning trades | Monthly sample review (1–2 per week) |
| Partial-fill or cancelled orders with meaningful near-miss impact | Yes — these are decisions too |

**Timing:** Complete the replay within 24 hours of closing the trade while memory is fresh. Do not wait until the weekly review for mandatory replays.

## Grading System Explained

### Grade A — Model Trade
- Every rule followed exactly as written in the trading plan
- Entry at or near the optimal zone
- Stop placed correctly, not moved unless rules allowed
- Exit at target or with valid rationale
- No emotional decisions
- Outcome doesn't matter for the grade — an A-grade trade can lose money and still be an A

**Action:** Log it. Study it. Replicate it.

### Grade B — Solid with Minor Issues
- Followed core rules, with 1–2 minor deviations that didn't materially harm the outcome
- Maybe entered slightly late, or took partial profit slightly early
- No emotional decisions or major rule breaks

**Action:** Note the specific B-level issue. Watch for it repeating. One B is fine; the same B three trades in a row is a pattern to fix.

### Grade C — Meaningful Violation
- Missed a rule that is clearly in the system
- Moved a stop because of hope (not rules)
- Sized up on a mediocre setup
- Let a winner turn to a loser by not taking profit at target
- Took a trade with only 2/5 required confluences

**Action:** Mandatory full replay. Identify the specific rule that was broken. Write it down in the lessons section. If the same C-grade mistake repeats 3+ times, it is a system-level problem requiring rule revision or a new practice protocol.

### Grade F — Unacceptable
- Traded without a stop
- Doubled down on a losing position without a pre-planned rule allowing it
- Revenge trade after a loss
- FOMO trade after missing a move
- Held through a major news event without hedging when rules say don't
- Violated position sizing rules (oversize)

**Action:** Mandatory full replay + journal entry about the emotional/psychological state that caused it. If two F-grades occur in the same week, consider a mandatory 2-day pause from trading to reset.

## Pattern Recognition Across Replays

The replay system is most powerful when you look for *patterns* across many trades.

### Monthly Replay Audit

At the start of each month, review the previous month's replays and answer:

```
MONTHLY REPLAY AUDIT

Total trades: ______
Grade distribution:
  A: ______ (___%)
  B: ______ (___%)
  C: ______ (___%)
  F: ______ (___%)

Most common C-grade mistake: ______
Most common F-grade trigger: ______

Win rate on A-grade trades: _____%
Win rate on B-grade trades: _____%
Win rate on C-grade trades: _____%
Win rate on F-grade trades: _____%

Pattern observed in losing trades: ______
Pattern observed in winning trades: ______

One rule change or addition based on this month's data:
→
```

The expectation over time: A and B grade trades should have a higher win rate than C and F grade trades. If C and F trades are profitable at the same rate as A trades, the grading criteria may need revision. If A and B trades are unprofitable, the underlying strategy (not execution) needs work.

## Using Replay Data to Evolve the System

Replay data is how ORACLE learns and upgrades its own rules.

**Valid system changes:**
- Observed pattern in 5+ replays where a specific entry condition leads to failure → add that as a filter
- Observed pattern where waiting for one more confirmation significantly improved outcomes → codify that requirement
- Observed that a specific exit rule consistently leaves large gains on the table → revise the rule

**Invalid reasons to change the system:**
- One bad trade (even an F) — not enough data
- Recent losing streak making you feel the rules don't work — check if the losses were rule-following A/B trades or C/F trades first
- A good feeling about a different approach — must be validated by at least 20 replay data points

## Replay Shortcuts (When Time Is Limited)

If a full replay is not possible within 24 hours, run a 5-minute "micro-replay":

```
MICRO-REPLAY: [TICKER] [DATE]

Setup: Valid / Forced?
Entry: By rules / Deviated?
Management: Disciplined / Reactive?
Exit: By rules / Emotional?
Key mistake (if any): 
Grade: A / B / C / F
Full replay needed? Y / N
```

A micro-replay is better than no replay. But mandatory C and F trades still need a full replay — complete it within 72 hours.
