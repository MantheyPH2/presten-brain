## Task 1: Unexplained Stale Team Investigation

**Assigned deliverable:** Root cause analysis of 127 unexplained stale teams.
**Delivered:** Full root cause breakdown with four categories, quantified counts, and structural diagnosis.

### What Was Delivered

The 127 teams were broken into four causal buckets:
- 41 teams (32%): Silent 200 / empty payload — GotSport returns HTTP 200 with `matches: []` on throttle; scraper cannot distinguish this from genuine no-game state
- 48 teams (38%): Queue dropout — worker-level `UNHANDLED_REJECTION` kills team IDs with no persistence to a dead letter queue
- 23 teams (18%): Parsing/upsert failure — non-standard GotSport ID prefix causes constraint violations logged silently at `debug` level
- 15 teams (12%): Genuine no-data — correctly identified as false positives in the stale audit query; these should be excluded from backfill

### Quality Assessment

**SENTINEL rating: Excellent.**

The categorization is specific enough to produce direct engineering actions. The 15 false-positive identification is important — without it, the backfill job would waste API capacity on teams that never had data. The observation that upsert failures are logged at `debug` rather than `warn` is a small catch with significant operational visibility implications.

The extension of the findings beyond the 127 specific teams — estimating 420-600 additional teams silently affected in the last 30 days — is the kind of second-order thinking SENTINEL looks for. The 127 are the visible symptom; the structural gap is the disease.

### Gaps / Concerns

None material. The one minor gap: the 23 non-standard GotSport ID prefix teams are identified but their specific prefix format is not documented. FORGE should document the exact prefix pattern in the pipeline fixes task so the fix can be validated against a known example.

## Task 2: Seasonal Team Archive Workflow

**Assigned deliverable:** Archive workflow design for the 180 de-prioritized teams.
**Delivered:** Production-grade design spec with SQL, pseudocode, pipeline integration, and safety guards.

### What Was Delivered

- `archived_at TIMESTAMP NULL` schema design with rationale for timestamp over boolean (auditable history)
- Full migration SQL with index
- Archival trigger criteria: three-condition AND logic with a cross-active team safety guard
- Complete candidate query in SQL with correct JOIN and HAVING logic
- Script pseudocode with dry-run capability, per-run audit log, and summary output
- Pipeline integration point (between Step 5 and Step 6) with rationale
- Schedule recommendation (weekly in season, monthly off-season) with a feature flag pattern
- Impact on the ranking engine's Glicko-2 RD inflation for concluded teams

### Quality Assessment

**SENTINEL rating: Excellent.**

The safety guard — excluding teams with any game in the last 90 days before archiving — is the right call and FORGE identified it independently. Without this guard, a team competing in a second active event could be incorrectly archived because their first event concluded. The guard is correctly written as a NOT EXISTS subquery.

The rationale for running the archive between Step 5 and Step 6 is sound. Running it after normalization (Steps 1-5) but before dedup (Step 6) gives the archive script the cleanest possible game history view and prevents archived teams from generating spurious dedup candidates.

The schema choice (nullable timestamp over boolean) is the right engineering decision and FORGE articulated the audit trail reason clearly.

### Gaps / Concerns

**One gap:** The candidate query has a subtle SQL issue. The GROUP BY clause includes `g.event_name` — this means a single team with games in multiple events would generate multiple rows (one per event_name). The intended behavior is one row per team. The HAVING clause applies to the grouped result, which could produce unexpected behavior when a team has games in both old and recent events. FORGE should validate this query against a test case where one team has games in both a concluded and an active event.

The gap does not undermine the design — it is a SQL correctness issue that will surface in testing. Flag it for the implementation task.

**Pending action item:** The schema migration is awaiting Presten's approval. FORGE correctly identified this and posted the approval request. No action needed from SENTINEL here — the queue item is appropriate.
