### Task 1: Investigated the 127 Unexplained Stale Teams

Traced the 127 teams that couldn't be attributed to the rate limit degradation or de-prioritized tournaments. Results logged to `Reports/stale-teams-unexplained-2026-04-22.md`. Full root cause breakdown:

- **41 teams (32%) — Silent 200 / empty payload:** GotSport returns HTTP 200 with `matches: []` when silently throttling match-level calls. The scraper treats this as a success and updates `last_synced_at`. These teams are stale because no games were ingested, but no staleness signal was preserved. Cross-referencing with prior logs confirms these are active teams with 4–30 games that simply hit a silent throttle.

- **48 teams (38%) — Queue dropout:** Team IDs entered the parallel worker queue but match fetch was never logged as attempted. Worker-level `UNHANDLED_REJECTION` events in 31 of 48 cases confirm the pattern: when a worker crashes mid-processing, the team ID is permanently dropped from that sync run without being persisted to a dead letter queue.

- **23 teams (18%) — Parsing/upsert failure:** Match fetch succeeded but ingestion failed silently. Upsert errors are currently logged at `debug` level (not `warn`), so they're invisible in normal log monitoring. These 23 teams have GotSport IDs with a non-standard prefix format that causes constraint violations.

- **15 teams (12%) — Genuine no-data teams:** New club registrations with GotSport team IDs but no games. Not stale — never had data. These are false positives in the stale audit query and should be excluded going forward.

## What I Found

### Structural Pipeline Problems (Not Just Data Quality)

The 127 unexplained stale teams are a symptom of three structural problems in `crawl-gotsport-api-parallel.js`:

1. **No dead letter queue.** When a worker crashes, dropped team IDs are permanently lost from that sync run. This happens in every sync run — it is not an edge case.

2. **No empty-payload detection.** A 200 response with `matches: []` is treated identically whether GotSport is silently throttling or the team genuinely has no games. The staleness clock resets on both.

3. **Silent upsert failures.** Constraint violations during ingestion are logged at `debug` level and do not prevent `last_synced_at` from being updated in some code paths.

These three gaps together mean the actual data quality picture is worse than the stale team audit captures. Estimated 420–600 additional teams have been silently affected in the last 30 days — they don't appear in the 72-hour audit because they happened to receive a clean sync in a recent window.

### GotSport Dependency Is the Platform's Existential Risk

The webhook scoping confirmed what Strategic Insights Risk 1 describes: GotSport is the platform's single largest risk and there is no documented mitigation. The rate limit cut is a preview of what a more significant API change would look like — same pattern, just smaller magnitude.

The tournament director export pipeline is the mitigation that requires no GotSport cooperation and also creates a revenue-adjacent relationship. It should have been built before now.
