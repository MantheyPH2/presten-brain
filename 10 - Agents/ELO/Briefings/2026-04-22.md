## What I Found

### The U13 Miscalibration Is Worse Than Yesterday's Briefing Indicated

Yesterday I flagged U13 as a "concern" and characterized its Brier score as suggesting potential overconfidence. SENTINEL was right to recharacterize it as a confirmed miscalibration. At 0.312, U13 is 35.6% above the acceptable threshold — this is not a minor calibration drift, it is a systematic failure for that age group. The root cause (combined RD floor + K-factor issue at the 11v11 developmental transition) is specific and tractable. The fix is clear.

### The MLS NEXT Academy Miscalibration Is Not Obvious But Is Real

The MLS NEXT error is structurally different from the Brier score issue — it is a league-level calibration problem, not an age-group parameter problem. The 25-point overcredit for Academy teams (160 vs justified ~135) means:
- Every ECNL team that beats an Academy team gets slightly less credit than they should
- Every Academy team that loses to ECNL gets slightly less penalized than they should
- Over thousands of cross-league games, this creates a systematic bias in the rating distribution for any team that plays MLS NEXT Academy opponents at showcases

This has likely been accumulating since MLS NEXT formalized the two-tier structure in 2025-26. The earlier you fix it, the less correction needed in 2026-27.

### The Migration Spec Has One Hairy Problem: Per-Team `birth_year_cohort` on Game Records

The `birth_year_cohort` column as originally envisioned (one value per game) works for historical data where all leagues were on birth-year cutoffs. But for 2026-27 cross-system games, a single game can legitimately involve teams from two different birth cohorts (e.g., a school-year U16 team whose players are born 2010 vs a birth-year U15 team whose players are born 2011). A single game-level `birth_year_cohort` column cannot capture this. The spec proposes `home_birth_year_cohort` and `away_birth_year_cohort` as the solution — worth highlighting for Presten's review before the schema change runs.
