## Tasks Completed

### 1. Ranking Engine Review (Elo/Glicko v7)
Read through the full engine spec and implementation notes in `Ranking/Engine/`. The v7 architecture uses a hybrid Elo/Glicko-2 approach: initial ratings use a simplified Elo K-factor model, and once a team has 10+ rated matches, the system transitions to Glicko-2 with full RD (rating deviation) and volatility tracking.

Notable observations:
- K-factor is currently set to 32 for all age groups regardless of competitive tier — this is a common simplification but may be inflating volatility for lower-tier recreational leagues where game outcomes are less predictive of true skill
- The Glicko-2 rating period is set to 30 days, which aligns well with typical tournament cadence
- New team provisional period (the first 10 matches under Elo before transitioning to Glicko-2) seems appropriate, though it creates a cliff in rating behavior at match 10 that could be smoothed
- Score margin is not factored into rating updates — a 1-0 win and a 5-0 win are treated identically. This is intentional per the v7 spec but worth revisiting for future versions

The engine logic itself is clean and well-documented. No bugs identified.
