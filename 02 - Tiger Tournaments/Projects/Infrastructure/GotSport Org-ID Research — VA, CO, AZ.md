### Virginia

**Association and league structure:**

- **Primary association:** Virginia Youth Soccer Association (VYSA) — the USYS state affiliate for Virginia
- **Note:** Virginia has two distinct youth soccer markets sharing state association governance: the Northern Virginia / DC-suburbs market (one of the densest youth soccer markets in the US — Arlington, Fairfax, Loudoun, Prince William counties) and the Richmond/Hampton Roads market.
- **Major leagues:** ODSL (Old Dominion Soccer League), multiple VYSA state leagues, VYSA State Cup, ECNL-Regional Southeast, NPL Southeast
- **Age groups:** U8–U19; primary pipeline interest is U11–U19 competitive brackets

**GotSport presence evidence:**

- VYSA manages Virginia State Cup via GotSport (event registration links documented on `vsysa.net` linking to `system.gotsport.com` event registration pages)
- Multiple VA club tournaments (Richmond Strikers, McLean Youth Soccer, etc.) are documented as GotSport-registered events
- The Northern VA market is heavily GotSport-centric — the same state-org pattern as Texas and Florida
- FORGE has not done a direct org_id browser lookup — "Likely" reflects the USYS P2 adoption pattern with VA-specific evidence of GotSport tournament registration

**Game volume estimate:**

- Virginia has approximately 100,000–120,000 registered youth soccer players (VYSA data, publicly reported)
- Estimate: ~8,000–15,000 competitive games/year through VYSA-affiliated events on GotSport
  - Sub-estimate: Northern VA alone accounts for 50–60% of volume (DC metro density)
  - This is comparable in scale to NJ (confirmed GotSport, in browser session queue)
- Historical archive likely spans 5+ years on GotSport — total reachable games may be 40,000–80,000

**Org-ID lookup status:**

- Org-ID not confirmed. Requires browser session: `system.gotsport.com` → search "Virginia Youth Soccer" or "VYSA"
- Expected: one primary org for VYSA. May have sub-org splits (Northern VA / Richmond) — confirm during browser session
- Add to Section 2 of `Infrastructure/GotSport Org-ID Master Reference — April 2026.md` when confirmed

### Colorado

**Association and league structure:**

- **Primary association:** Colorado Youth Soccer Association (CYSA) — the USYS state affiliate
- **Note:** Colorado Soccer Association is a single statewide body (unlike CA or NY which have geographic splits). The Front Range (Denver metro + Boulder + Fort Collins + Colorado Springs) accounts for the majority of registered players.
- **Major leagues:** Colorado Soccer League (CSL), Rocky Mountain Cup, ECNL-Regional Southwest, CYSA State Cup
- **Age groups:** U8–U19; primary pipeline interest is U11–U19 competitive brackets

**GotSport presence evidence:**

- CYSA is confirmed in the GotSport USYS Org-ID Master List as a GotSport platform user (P2 priority, "Yes" classification)
- Colorado State Cup and CSL events appear on GotSport event search results — public event registration URLs contain `system.gotsport.com` paths
- The Front Range youth soccer market is a growing national club hub — Colorado Rush, Colorado Rapids youth program, and Real Colorado are nationally prominent clubs. All are documented as GotSport users.
- CYSA adoption of GotSport is consistent with surrounding Western states (WA and CA both confirmed GotSport)

**Game volume estimate:**

- Colorado has approximately 65,000–80,000 registered youth soccer players
- Estimate: ~5,000–10,000 competitive games/year through CYSA-affiliated events on GotSport
  - Colorado has a compressed season (spring March–June, fall Aug–Oct) due to altitude/weather; year-round play is less common than AZ
  - Volume is consistent with NJ estimate (~5,000–9,000 games/year)
- Historical archive: estimated 4–6 years of GotSport data → 20,000–60,000 total reachable games

**Org-ID lookup status:**

- Org-ID not confirmed. Requires browser session: `system.gotsport.com` → search "Colorado Youth Soccer" or "CYSA"
- Expected: one primary org for CYSA (no known sub-state split unlike CA or TX)
- Add to Section 2 of `Infrastructure/GotSport Org-ID Master Reference — April 2026.md` when confirmed

### Arizona

**Association and league structure:**

- **Primary association:** Arizona Youth Soccer (AzSA) — the USYS state affiliate. Also operates as Arizona Soccer Association.
- **Note:** Arizona features **year-round play** — the warm, dry climate allows competition from September through May with a peak during the winter months (avoiding summer heat). This inflates annual game volume significantly relative to states with harsh winters.
- **Major leagues:** Arizona Soccer Association leagues (Metro, Metro Youth), AzSA State Cup, ECNL-Regional Southwest, multiple club leagues
- **Age groups:** U8–U19; year-round competition means higher per-team game counts than northern states

**GotSport presence evidence:**

- Arizona Soccer Association is listed in the GotSport USYS Org-ID Master List as "GotSport: Yes (likely)" (P2 priority)
- AzSA-affiliated tournaments (Fiesta Bowl Soccer, Desert Cup, Scottsdale Soccer) appear in GotSport event searches
- The Phoenix metro area (Maricopa County) is one of the largest youth soccer markets in the Southwest — a major GotSport adoption market
- Club programs (AZ Fire, Scottsdale Blackhawks, FC Phoenix) are documented GotSport users at the event level

**Game volume estimate:**

- Arizona has approximately 60,000–75,000 registered youth soccer players
- Estimate: ~5,000–12,000 competitive games/year through AzSA-affiliated events on GotSport
  - Year-round play premium: AZ teams play roughly 25–35% more games per year than northern states (no weather shutdown)
  - The higher end of the range (12,000) applies if historical seasonal archives are captured
- Historical archive: estimated 4–6 years → 20,000–70,000 total reachable games (higher per-season volume)

**Org-ID lookup status:**

- Org-ID not confirmed. Requires browser session: `system.gotsport.com` → search "Arizona Youth Soccer" or "Arizona Soccer Association"
- Expected: one primary org for AzSA. No known sub-state split.
- Add to Section 2 of `Infrastructure/GotSport Org-ID Master Reference — April 2026.md` when confirmed
