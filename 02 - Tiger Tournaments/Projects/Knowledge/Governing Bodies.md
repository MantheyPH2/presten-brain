# Governing Bodies

US youth soccer is governed by two main organizations with fundamentally different structures. Understanding the distinction is important for how leagues and events are organized.

---

## US Club Soccer

| Attribute      | Value                                          |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Model          | **Direct national membership** — clubs join directly |
| Key leagues    | [[ECNL]], NPL                                 |
| Focus          | Elite competitive pathway                      |
| Structure      | National office → clubs (no state layer)       |

- Clubs register directly with US Club Soccer at the national level
- Operates [[ECNL]] (boys + girls) and NPL as its flagship platforms
- More streamlined governance with fewer bureaucratic layers
- Generally serves higher-level competitive clubs

---

## US Youth Soccer (USYS)

| Attribute       | Value                                          |
| --------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Model           | **State-association-based** — clubs join through their state |
| State associations | 55                                          |
| Clubs           | 10,000+                                       |
| Players         | 2.68 million                                  |
| Key programs    | State Cups, ODP, National League               |

- Clubs register through their **state association**, which then affiliates with USYS
- 55 state associations create a federated structure
- Covers the broadest range of competitive levels from recreational to elite
- Runs State Cup championships, ODP (Olympic Development Program), and the National League
- USYS National League integrating with NPL for 2026-27

---

## Key Difference

> [!info] Direct vs State-Based
> The fundamental architectural difference:
> - **US Club Soccer:** Club → National Organization (direct)
> - **USYS:** Club → State Association → National Organization (federated)
>
> This affects how events are sanctioned, how teams register for tournaments, and how player passes work. A [[GotSport]]-hosted tournament may require either US Club Soccer or USYS membership depending on the sanctioning body.

---

## Impact on Evo Draw

- Events may be sanctioned by either body — affects which teams can participate
- Some clubs hold dual membership (both US Club Soccer and USYS)
- [[League Hierarchy]] spans both governing bodies:
  - US Club Soccer: [[ECNL]], [[ECNL RL]], NPL
  - USYS: State Cups, ODP, National League
  - Independent: [[MLS NEXT]], GA, USL Academy
