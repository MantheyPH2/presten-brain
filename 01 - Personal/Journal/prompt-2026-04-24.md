# Journal Prompt — April 24, 2026

## Today's Prompt

> **In 5 days, the largest earnings week of the year hits. MSFT, META, AMZN, GOOGL all report April 29. PCE prints April 30. Every thesis you've built gets tested in a 48-hour window. The research is mostly done. The question now is: what will you actually DO when the moment arrives? Think about a time you prepared well for something and then froze — or failed to execute when it mattered. What was the gap between the preparation and the execution? What would you tell yourself to close it?**

---

## Expansion Questions

1. You know the thesis. You know the risk. You know the entry plan. The hardest part is actually placing the trade when the moment comes. What specifically makes you hesitate — not theoretically, but in the moment right before a decision? Is it fear of being wrong in public? Fear of the loss itself? Something else?

2. SEC's March 2026 taxonomy removed 8 years of regulatory uncertainty for crypto. Projects that have been building silently through the gray zone are now operating in the clear. Where in your own life are you waiting for "permission" or "clarity" that you've already been given — and you just haven't acted on it?

3. The QQQ bear put spread must be entered April 28 — not April 30, not the morning of. The optimal setup requires positioning BEFORE the event, not during it. Where in your non-financial life are you repeatedly missing the "April 28" moment — the window to act before the catalyst arrives — because you're waiting to see how things develop?

4. Every position in the journal is marked PENDING. Paper trades are real practice, but there's a mental accounting trick where "pending" feels reversible. If every current paper trade were a real trade that you couldn't change — would you hold them all with the same conviction? Or are there positions you'd close right now if real money was on the line?

5. Intel's CEO said the foundry "fails without external customers." He said this publicly, on the record, before the Tesla deal. He committed before he had the win. Where in your life could you create more momentum by stating a commitment before you have the result — not boasting, but genuine public accountability that changes your behavior?

## Reflection Anchor

*The research phase is a form of safety. As long as you're still "preparing," you can't be wrong yet. At some point, preparation becomes delay. The traders who make money aren't the ones with the best models — they're the ones who execute on a good model when the moment arrives. Five days. The moment is coming. Are you actually ready to act, or are you still "preparing"?*
