# Journal Prompt — April 23, 2026

## Today's Prompt

> **Today the market rewarded hardware (TXN +18%, INTC +19%) and punished software that couldn't justify its price (IBM -8%, ServiceNow -17%). The pattern was ruthless and clean. What is your own equivalent of this? Where in your life are you a hardware play — producing real, measurable output that earns its valuation — and where are you priced for perfection while actually coasting? What would happen if the market repriced you today?**

---

## Expansion Questions

1. The market doesn't care about effort — only results. Where in your work or life are you doing the effort but not producing the output that actually matters? What's the gap between your activity level and your output quality?

2. Intel's CEO bet the entire foundry division on winning one external customer. He got Tesla. What's the big bet you're avoiding making because you're afraid of needing that one external validation? What's your Tesla?

3. BTC was unmoved today while tech bled. It's found its own floor — not correlated to anyone else's panic. Where in your thinking have you built that kind of independence? Where are you still correlated to other people's anxiety and opinions?

4. Every analyst who upgraded AMZN to $298-$325 has a number. They committed. What are your actual numbers — the specific, uncomfortable targets you've committed to, not the vague aspirations you can always reframe as "on track"?

5. The hardest part of a trade isn't finding the thesis — it's holding through the noise without either panic-selling or losing discipline. Where in your current projects are you in the "hold through the noise" phase? Is your conviction thesis still intact, or have you been slowly lowering your expectations without admitting it?
